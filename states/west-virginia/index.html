Correct: B Licensed brokers with 10+ years experience

At least three of the five WVREC commissioners must be licensed real estate brokers who have been actively engaged in the profession for at least 10 years.

What types of administrative sanctions can the WVREC impose on a licensee?

  1. A Fines, suspension, or revocation
  2. B Only written warnings for first offenses
  3. C Only fines, no license action
  4. D Only license revocation, no fines
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Fines, suspension, or revocation

The WVREC can impose a range of administrative sanctions including fines, license suspension, and license revocation. The commission has flexibility to impose one or more sanctions as appropriate.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which WV license type allows the holder to operate independently and supervise salespersons?

  1. A Broker
  2. B Managing agent
  3. C Salesperson
  4. D Associate broker
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Broker

Only a broker can operate independently and supervise salespersons in WV. An associate broker holds broker qualifications but works under another broker, similar to a salesperson.

Q5 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under WV law, which activity requires a real estate license when performed for compensation?

  1. A Installing a fence on private property
  2. B Mowing a rental property's lawn
  3. C Negotiating the sale of real estate
  4. D Painting a house for a homeowner
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Negotiating the sale of real estate

Negotiating the sale, exchange, purchase, or rental of real estate for compensation requires a license. Physical property maintenance does not require a license.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

A WV salesperson is asked to prepare a deed for a client. Can the salesperson legally do this?

  1. A No, that constitutes unauthorized practice of law
  2. B Yes, with a disclaimer that it is not legal advice
  3. C Yes, if the broker approves
  4. D Yes, for standard residential transactions
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A No, that constitutes unauthorized practice of law

Licensees may fill in blanks on approved forms but may not draft legal documents or give legal advice. Preparing a deed is the practice of law and requires an attorney.

All advertising must include the broker's name as registered with the WVREC. This applies to all forms of advertising including print, online, social media, yard signs, and business cards.

The WVREC can impose administrative fines and license suspension or revocation. Criminal prosecution is handled separately through the court system; the commission does not award damages.

An associate broker holds broker qualifications (education and experience) but chooses to work under another broker rather than operating independently. A salesperson has only salesperson-level qualifications.

FAQ

West Virginia real estate exam — questions answered

How many questions are on the West Virginia real estate exam?

The West Virginia salesperson exam has 130 multiple-choice questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 50-question state portion. You get 210 minutes in total, and you need 70% on each portion to pass.

What's the passing score for the West Virginia real estate exam?

You need 70% on each portion separately. The national and state sections are scored independently, so you have to clear both. You receive a score report marked pass or fail at the test center.

How much does the West Virginia real estate exam cost?

The examination fee is $59 per exam part, paid to Pearson VUE when you reserve your seat. Because the salesperson exam has a national part and a state part, plan for that fee on each part you need to take.

How long is the West Virginia real estate exam?

You get 150 minutes for the 80-question national portion and 60 minutes for the 50-question state portion, 210 minutes in all. That is a little over a minute per question, so a steady pace matters.

Who administers the West Virginia real estate exam?

The West Virginia Real Estate Salesperson Exam is administered by Pearson VUE for the West Virginia Real Estate Commission. You register and schedule your exam date through Pearson VUE.

Is the West Virginia real estate exam hard?

It is challenging, especially the state portion. Many candidates handle the national real estate principles fine but underestimate how much West Virginia-specific law the state section covers across its 50 questions. Focused state prep is what separates first-time passers from retakes.

What's on the West Virginia real estate salesperson exam?

The national portion covers property ownership, land use controls, valuation, financing, agency, property disclosures, contracts, leasing and property management, transfer of title, the practice of real estate, and real estate calculations. The state portion focuses on the duties and powers of the West Virginia Real Estate Commission, the statutes that govern licensee activity, agency-disclosure rules, broker trust-account requirements, and West Virginia fair housing law.

What's the best way to prepare for the West Virginia real estate exam?

Practice with West Virginia-specific questions until you score above 80% consistently, then keep drilling the topics you miss most. Review every explanation, not just the ones you get wrong, and use practice tests that mirror the real exam's split national-and-state format and difficulty.
