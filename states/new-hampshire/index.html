Which state agency oversees the NH Real Estate Commission?

  1. A Department of Justice consumer protection division
  2. B Office of Professional Licensure and Certification
  3. C Secretary of State professional licensing bureau
  4. D Department of Revenue Administration office
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Office of Professional Licensure and Certification

The NH Real Estate Commission operates under OPLC (Office of Professional Licensure and Certification). OPLC was restructured in 2023 under HB 655, consolidating professional licensing oversight.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What bond must a NH principal broker maintain as a condition of licensure?

  1. A A $10,000 surety bond
  2. B A $50,000 surety bond
  3. C A $100,000 surety bond
  4. D A $25,000 surety bond
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D A $25,000 surety bond

NH requires each principal broker to maintain a $25,000 surety bond. This bond replaced the recovery fund (repealed September 1, 2023) as the primary consumer protection mechanism for real estate transactions.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under RSA 331-A:26, which conduct is prohibited for NH licensees?

  1. A Representing commercial buyers
  2. B Advertising on social media
  3. C Misrepresentation in a transaction
  4. D Charging a flat-fee commission
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Misrepresentation in a transaction

RSA 331-A:26 lists prohibited conduct including misrepresentation, undisclosed dual agency, commingling, failure to account, paying unlicensed persons, inducing breach of contract, guaranteeing profits, and advertising violations.

Q4 of 20 State Practice

Under RSA 331-A:13, where must a principal broker maintain the trust account?

  1. A In an out-of-state escrow company
  2. B In the broker's personal account
  3. C In any federally insured bank
  4. D In a separate NH bank account
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D In a separate NH bank account

RSA 331-A:13 requires that the trust account be maintained as a separate account in a New Hampshire bank. This ensures that client funds are identifiable, accessible to regulators, and subject to NH jurisdiction.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

A licensee posts a property listing on social media without mentioning the brokerage. Under Rea 404.05, what type of ad is this?

  1. A A compliant digital ad
  2. B An exempt personal post
  3. C A prohibited blind ad
  4. D A permissible team ad
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C A prohibited blind ad

Under Rea 404.05, all advertisements must include the brokerage name as licensed. An ad that omits the brokerage identification is a blind ad and is prohibited regardless of the medium, including social media.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

Under Rea 701.03, which detail must be disclosed about a property's water supply?

  2. B Name of the water company
  3. C Most recent water test date
  4. D Monthly water bill amount

Correct: C Most recent water test date

Rea 701.03 requires disclosure of the water supply type, location, any malfunctions, installation date, and the most recent test date. Monthly bill amounts and pressure readings are not specified requirements.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

Under RSA 331-A, how many types of brokerage relationships are recognized in New Hampshire?

  1. A Five types
  2. B Three types
  3. C Six types
  4. D Four types
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Five types

RSA 331-A recognizes five types of brokerage relationships: seller agent (25-b), buyer agent (25-c), disclosed dual agent (25-d), designated agent (25-e), and facilitator (25-f).

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

Under RSA 356-B, how many units trigger the Public Offering Statement registration requirement when marketed to the public?

  1. A More than 10 units
  2. B Twenty-five or more units
  3. C Two or more units
  4. D Five or more units
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A More than 10 units

RSA 356-B:49 exempts a condominium from registration and Public Offering Statement requirements if not more than 10 units are included in the project (unless time-sharing interests are involved). The threshold for triggering full registration is therefore more than 10 units. A 'small condominium' of 25 or fewer units may still qualify for abbreviated registration under RSA 356-B:51.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

What is the NH real estate transfer tax rate per party under RSA 78-B?

  1. A $0.50 per $100 of sale price
  2. B $0.75 per $100 of sale price
  3. C $1.00 per $100 of sale price
  4. D $1.50 per $100 of sale price
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B $0.75 per $100 of sale price

Under RSA 78-B, the NH transfer tax is $0.75 per $100 of the sale price, paid by each party (buyer and seller). The combined rate equals 1.5% of the sale price.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

What is the default notice period for eviction in NH under RSA 540?

  1. A 30 days
  2. B 7 days
  3. C 14 days
  4. D 60 days
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A 30 days

RSA 540 establishes a default 30-day notice period for eviction in New Hampshire. Shorter periods apply for nonpayment, damage, or non-compliance (7 days), and longer periods for non-renewal of 12+ month leases (60 days).

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

About the exam

More about the New Hampshire real estate exam

The New Hampshire Real Estate Commission (NHREC) grades the 40-question state portion on its own, so a strong national score won't rescue a weak state score. That makes New Hampshire-specific preparation matter as much as the national material.

New Hampshire repealed its Real Estate Recovery Fund effective September 1, 2023, and shifted consumer protection to a $25,000 principal-broker surety bond under RSA 331-A:14. The state recognizes five distinct brokerage relationships under RSA 331-A:25-b through :25-f, including the facilitator role that very few states use. RSA 477:4-c forces sellers to disclose private water-supply and sewage information before contract, the RSA 78-B transfer tax splits $0.75 per $100 between buyer and seller, and RSA 354-A's Law Against Discrimination piles state-specific protected classes on top of federal fair housing. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need New Hampshire-specific practice questions.

FAQ

New Hampshire real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the New Hampshire real estate exam?

The New Hampshire Real Estate Salesperson Examination has 120 scored multiple-choice questions. PSI splits the test into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question New Hampshire portion, and they are scored separately.

What's the passing score for the New Hampshire real estate exam?

70% on each portion, scored independently. That works out to at least 56 of 80 correct on the national portion and at least 28 of 40 correct on the New Hampshire portion. You have to clear both bars in the same sitting, so a strong national score will not rescue a weak state score.

How much does the New Hampshire real estate exam cost?

$67 for a first attempt and $65 for any retake, paid to PSI when you schedule. That's separate from the New Hampshire Office of Professional Licensure and Certification license fee you'll pay after you pass.

How long is the New Hampshire real estate exam?

Four hours total. PSI gives you 150 minutes for the 80 national questions and 90 minutes for the 40 New Hampshire questions, and you find out whether you passed each portion before you leave the test center.

Who administers the New Hampshire real estate exam?

The New Hampshire Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered by PSI for the New Hampshire Real Estate Commission. You register and schedule your exam date through PSI.

Is the New Hampshire real estate exam hard?

Most candidates underestimate the state portion. The national side is the same shared content New Hampshire candidates see in every state, and a decent prep course will get you there. The 40 state questions are where people lose. You're tested on the 2023 Recovery Fund repeal and the $25,000 principal-broker surety bond, the five brokerage relationships in RSA 331-A:25-b through :25-f including New Hampshire's facilitator role, RSA 477:4-c water and sewage disclosure, the RSA 78-B transfer tax, RSA 354-A's Law Against Discrimination, RSA 205-A manufactured-housing-park rights, and the NHREC's Rea 400 and Rea 700 administrative rules on advertising, trust accounts, and disclosure timing. Honest practice on New Hampshire-specific material is the difference between passing on your first $67 attempt and retaking.

What's on the New Hampshire real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers property ownership, land use, financing, agency and fiduciary duties, valuation, contracts, transfer of title, practice of real estate, and basic real estate math. The 40-question New Hampshire portion tests the Real Estate Commission and OPLC, RSA 331-A licensee conduct and the post-2023 surety-bond framework, the NHREC's Rea administrative rules (advertising under Rea 404.05, trust accounts and disclosure timing under Rea 701 and 702), the five RSA 331-A:25 brokerage relationships including disclosed dual agency and the facilitator role, RSA 477:4-c property condition disclosures, RSA 78-B transfer tax, RSA 354-A fair housing, RSA 540 landlord-tenant practice, RSA 356-B condominium registration, RSA 205-A manufactured-housing-park tenant rights, and New Hampshire planning and zoning under RSA 672 and RSA 674.
