Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A Governor appointee to WREC has been a licensee for 3 years. Does this person qualify for appointment?

  1. A No, 10+ years of experience is required
  2. B Yes, any active licensee qualifies
  3. C Yes, if confirmed by the Senate
  4. D No, 5+ years of experience is required
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D No, 5+ years of experience is required

Wyoming law requires that the 3 to 4 licensee members of WREC must have a minimum of 5 years of active licensure experience. A person with only 3 years does not meet the statutory qualification, regardless of Senate confirmation.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which license type allows a person to operate independently without a supervising broker in Wyoming?

  1. A Associate license
  2. B Salesperson license
  3. C Apprentice license
  4. D Broker license
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Broker license

In Wyoming, a broker license permits independent practice without supervision. A salesperson must work under a responsible broker at all times. The responsible broker is the designated broker for a company who oversees all licensee activities within that firm.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A salesperson is terminated by their broker. What happens to the salesperson's license?

  1. A The license becomes inactive immediately
  2. B The salesperson becomes an independent broker
  3. C The license is automatically cancelled
  4. D The license stays active for 30 days
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A The license becomes inactive immediately

When a salesperson is terminated by their broker in Wyoming, the license becomes inactive until it is transferred to a new broker. A salesperson cannot practice without a responsible broker affiliation, so the license remains inactive until a new broker transfer is completed with WREC.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which practice is explicitly prohibited in Wyoming real estate transactions?

  1. A Dual agency representation
  2. B Transaction brokerage
  3. C Exclusive buyer agency
  4. D Seller-only representation
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Dual agency representation

Wyoming is one of the few states that prohibits dual agency entirely. A licensee cannot represent both the buyer and the seller in the same transaction. This prohibition exists to prevent the inherent conflict of interest that arises when one agent attempts to serve two parties with opposing interests.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

A Wyoming buyer hires a licensee as their agent. Which duty does the agent owe?

  1. A Disclosure of material facts only
  2. B Honesty and fair dealing only
  3. C Accounting and confidentiality only
  4. D Full fiduciary duties
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Full fiduciary duties

Under W.S. §33-28-301–306, an agent in Wyoming owes full fiduciary duties including loyalty, obedience, disclosure, confidentiality, accounting, and reasonable care. This is the highest level of duty owed in any of the three relationship types.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

When must a Wyoming licensee provide the agency disclosure form to a prospective buyer?

  1. A At closing or settlement
  2. B At the time of the first written offer
  3. C Within 48 hours of the first showing
  4. D Before any discussion about a property
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Before any discussion about a property

Wyoming law requires written agency disclosure before any discussion or arrangement incidental to a sale, purchase, exchange, or lease. This ensures the consumer understands the relationship types and duties before substantive conversations begin.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

A Wyoming broker receives earnest money on Friday afternoon. By when must it be deposited?

  1. A Within 48 hours of receipt
  2. B Within 3 business days
  3. C The next banking day
  4. D By the following Friday
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C The next banking day

Wyoming requires trust funds to be deposited no later than the first banking day after receipt. If received Friday afternoon, the deposit must be made by Monday (assuming Monday is a banking day). This is stricter than most states' deposit timelines.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

A Wyoming salesperson posts a property listing on social media using only her personal name. What violation has occurred?

  1. A A blind ad without broker identification
  2. B Using an unapproved marketing platform
  3. C Failure to obtain a social media license
  4. D Advertising without a written contract
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A A blind ad without broker identification

Wyoming requires the broker's licensed name or registered trade name to appear in ALL advertising, including social media. An ad showing only the salesperson's name without broker identification is a prohibited blind ad under WREC rules.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

A local real estate association publishes a suggested commission rate schedule for all member brokers. What type of violation does this represent?

  1. A A breach of fiduciary duty
  2. B Antitrust price-fixing
  3. C An unauthorized practice of law
  4. D A WREC advertising violation
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Antitrust price-fixing

Setting or suggesting commission rates among competing brokers constitutes price-fixing, a per se violation of federal antitrust law. Commission rates must always be freely negotiable between the broker and the client. No organization may establish standard rates.

Q10 of 20 State Practice

What is the minimum E&O insurance coverage required per claim for Wyoming licensees?

  1. A $500,000 per claim
  2. B $100,000 per claim
  3. C $250,000 per claim
  4. D $50,000 per claim
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B $100,000 per claim

Wyoming requires all active licensees to maintain errors and omissions insurance with a minimum of $100,000 per claim and $500,000 aggregate coverage under W.S. 33-28-401. This has been mandatory since January 1, 2008.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

How is Wyoming's real estate recovery account funded?

  1. A Annual appropriations from the general fund
  2. B A $20 fee from each license issuance or renewal
  3. C A one-time $100 fee at initial licensure only
  4. D A percentage of each commission earned
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B A $20 fee from each license issuance or renewal

Wyoming funds its recovery account through a $20 fee collected every time a person obtains or renews a real estate license. The fee is not based on commissions, general fund appropriations, or a one-time charge.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

A Wyoming landlord asks what the maximum security deposit is under state law. The correct answer is:

  1. A Three months' rent for furnished units
  2. B There is no statutory maximum in Wyoming
  3. C Two months' rent for all rental units
  4. D One month's rent for unfurnished units
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B There is no statutory maximum in Wyoming

Wyoming does not impose a statutory cap on security deposit amounts. This is one of several provisions that make Wyoming considered landlord-friendly. The amount is left to negotiation between landlord and tenant.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

Which foreclosure methods are available in Wyoming?

  1. A Strict foreclosure without any sale
  2. B Judicial and non-judicial foreclosure
  3. C Only non-judicial foreclosure methods
  4. D Only judicial foreclosure methods
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Judicial and non-judicial foreclosure

Wyoming permits both judicial foreclosure through court proceedings with a sheriff sale, and non-judicial foreclosure using the power of sale clause. The method depends on the terms of the security instrument.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

What is the primary method of land description used in Wyoming?

  1. A Rectangular (government) survey system
  2. B GPS coordinate-based descriptions
  3. C Metes and bounds descriptions
  4. D Lot and block from recorded plats
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Rectangular (government) survey system

Due to Wyoming's vast open geography and western settlement patterns, the rectangular (government) survey system using townships and ranges is the primary method of land description. Metes and bounds is used in some areas but is not the dominant method.

WREC receives a consumer complaint about a licensee's advertising. What investigative authority does the commission have?

  1. A It may investigate and examine broker records
  2. B It must wait for a court order to investigate
  3. C It must refer the matter to local police
  4. D It can only investigate if the broker consents

Correct: A It may investigate and examine broker records

WREC has broad investigative powers including the authority to investigate complaints, examine broker records, and audit trust accounts at any time. The commission does not need broker consent or a court order to exercise these powers.

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A property owner pays a friend $500 to find a tenant for a rental property. The friend is unlicensed. Is this legal in Wyoming?

  1. A Yes, payments under $1,000 are exempt
  2. B No, leasing for compensation requires licensure
  3. C Yes, friends are exempt from license laws
  4. D Yes, residential rentals are always exempt
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B No, leasing for compensation requires licensure

Wyoming law requires a real estate license for anyone who engages in listing, selling, buying, exchanging, leasing, or property management for compensation. Receiving $500 to find a tenant is leasing for compensation and requires licensure, regardless of the personal relationship.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A salesperson changes broker affiliations. What notification must occur?

  1. A The salesperson files with Pearson VUE
  2. B Only the new broker files with the county
  3. C WREC must be notified of the change
  4. D No notification is required
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C WREC must be notified of the change

When a salesperson changes broker affiliation in Wyoming, WREC must be notified of the change. This ensures the commission maintains accurate records of which licensees are affiliated with which brokers, which is essential for supervision accountability and consumer protection.

Q18 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker deposits a client's earnest money into the broker's personal checking account. What violation has occurred?

  1. A Unauthorized dual agency
  2. B Practicing without a license
  3. C Commingling of trust funds
  4. D Failure to maintain E&O insurance
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Commingling of trust funds

Depositing client funds into a personal account constitutes commingling, which is a serious violation of Wyoming real estate law. Client funds must be deposited into a properly maintained trust or escrow account, kept separate from the broker's personal or business operating funds.

Q19 of 20 State Practice

A seller contacts a brokerage but does not sign a listing agreement. What relationship exists?

  1. A Intermediary relationship
  2. B Customer relationship
  3. C No legal relationship exists
  4. D Agent relationship
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Customer relationship

Without a signed agreement establishing agency, the seller is treated as a customer. A customer is owed only honesty and fair dealing. No fiduciary duties attach until an agency agreement is created in writing.

About the exam

More about the Wyoming real estate exam

The Wyoming real estate salesperson exam trips up more first-time candidates than they expect. You have to pass the national and Wyoming portions on their own, so a strong national score won't make up for a weak showing on Wyoming law, and that is where unprepared candidates lose the most points. That makes Wyoming-specific preparation matter as much as the national material.

Wyoming tests the powers and disciplinary process of the Real Estate Commission, the Real Estate Recovery and Education Fund, the rules that govern how licensees advertise and handle documents and money, the agency relationships and disclosures a salesperson owes the parties, and mandatory errors and omissions insurance. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need Wyoming-specific practice questions.

FAQ

Wyoming real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Wyoming real estate exam?

The Wyoming salesperson exam has 120 scored multiple-choice questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question Wyoming portion. A handful of unscored pretest questions are mixed in that don't count toward your result. Pearson VUE administers it for the Wyoming Real Estate Commission, and a first-time candidate has to pass both portions to get licensed.

What's the passing score for the Wyoming real estate exam?

Wyoming reports your result as a scaled score from 0 to 100, with 75 set as the passing score on each portion. A scaled 75 reflects how your performance compares across different versions of the exam rather than a flat 75% of the questions correct. The national and Wyoming portions are scored separately, so you have to clear both.

How much does the Wyoming real estate exam cost?

The examination fee is $80 per portion at a Pearson VUE test center, so a first-time candidate sitting both the national and Wyoming portions pays $160 in total. Testing through OnVUE online proctoring instead runs $90 per portion. The fee is paid to Pearson VUE when you reserve your seat and isn't accepted at the test center.

How long is the Wyoming real estate exam?

You get 2.5 hours for the 80-question national portion and 1.5 hours for the 40-question Wyoming portion, four hours in all. First-time candidates take both portions, and you leave the test center with an official score report marked pass or fail.

Who administers the Wyoming real estate exam?

The Wyoming Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered by Pearson VUE for the Wyoming Real Estate Commission. You register and schedule your exam date through Pearson VUE.

Is the Wyoming real estate exam hard?

It is challenging, and the Wyoming portion is where most candidates slip. Many handle national topics like financing and valuation well, then underestimate how much Wyoming-specific law the 40-question state portion packs in across Commission powers, licensee conduct rules, and agency disclosures. Because you pass or fail each portion on its own, a strong national score can't rescue a weak state score. Focused practice on Wyoming law is what separates first-time passers from retakes.

What's on the Wyoming real estate salesperson exam?

The 80-question national portion covers real property characteristics and land use, forms of ownership and transfer of title, property value and appraisal, contracts and agency, the practice of real estate, property and environmental disclosures, financing and settlement, and real estate math. The 40-question Wyoming portion focuses on the powers of the Real Estate Commission, the rules that govern licensee activities like advertising and the handling of documents and money, agency relationships and disclosures, the Real Estate Recovery and Education Fund, and additional topics like land description, landlord and tenant law, foreclosure and redemption, and broker price opinions.
