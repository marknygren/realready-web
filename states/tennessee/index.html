Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under whom must a Tennessee affiliate broker work?

  1. A A principal broker
  2. B A managing associate broker
  3. C Any licensed broker in the state
  4. D The TREC executive director
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A A principal broker

Tennessee affiliate brokers must work under a principal broker, the supervising broker of a firm. The principal broker oversees the affiliate's activities and ensures compliance with all laws.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What type of insurance does Tennessee require all real estate licensees to maintain?

  1. A Professional malpractice insurance
  2. B Errors and omissions insurance
  3. C Surety bond coverage
  4. D General liability insurance
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Errors and omissions insurance

Tennessee requires all licensees to maintain Errors and Omissions (E&O) insurance. E&O specifically covers claims from professional negligence, errors, or omissions in licensed activities.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under T.C.A. § 62-13-312, which action is grounds for discipline by TREC?

  1. A Refusing to list a property below market value
  2. B Commingling trust funds with personal funds
  3. C Charging a commission rate above 6% to sellers
  4. D Advertising a listed property at market value
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Commingling trust funds with personal funds

Commingling trust funds with personal or business funds is a serious violation under the License Act. Agents must keep client funds separate in a designated trust account.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

In Tennessee, how is an agency relationship between a licensee and a client created?

  1. A Only by a written bilateral agreement
  2. B Automatically at first substantive contact
  3. C By delivering a written agency disclosure form
  4. D By the licensee's conduct or verbal statements
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Only by a written bilateral agreement

Under T.C.A. § 62-13-401, a Tennessee agency relationship cannot be assumed, implied, or created by a licensee's conduct or statements. It exists only through a written bilateral agreement, such as a listing agreement or a buyer representation agreement. Delivering an agency disclosure or confirmation form does not by itself create agency, and until an agreement is signed the licensee is a facilitator.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

What is required before a Tennessee licensee can act as a dual agent?

  3. C TREC approval of the arrangement
  4. D Only the seller's written consent
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Informed written consent from both parties

Dual agency requires informed written consent from both the buyer and seller. Both parties must understand and agree to the agent representing both sides.

Correct: B Only through their principal broker

Tennessee law requires that affiliate brokers receive all compensation through their principal broker. Direct payment from clients, customers, or other brokers is prohibited.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

Where must a Tennessee principal broker hold trust money received in a transaction?

  1. A In any account, as long as the broker keeps accurate records
  2. B In a TREC-operated state escrow account
  3. C In a separate, federally insured escrow or trust account
  4. D In the principal broker's personal bank account
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C In a separate, federally insured escrow or trust account

Under TREC Rule 1260-02-.09, each principal broker must hold trust money in a separate escrow or trustee account, in the name of the firm, at a federally insured institution. Tennessee does not require the bank to be located in Tennessee. What matters is that the account is separate and the funds are never commingled with personal or business funds.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

What is Tennessee's position on net listings?

  1. A Required for commercial properties
  2. B Discouraged but allowed with written disclosure
  3. C Encouraged as a standard practice
  4. D Prohibited by TREC rule
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Prohibited by TREC rule

Net listings are prohibited in Tennessee. TREC Rule 1260-02-.07 states that no broker or affiliate broker shall accept or enter a listing based on a 'net price.' Net listings create a conflict of interest because the broker keeps everything above the seller's net figure, so Tennessee bans them outright rather than merely discouraging them.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

Does Tennessee require the use of state-mandated contract forms for real estate transactions?

  3. C Yes, all contracts must use TAR forms
  4. D Only for properties over $250,000

Correct: B No, Tennessee does not mandate specific forms

Unlike Texas, Tennessee does not require state-mandated contract forms. The Tennessee Association of Realtors (TAR) publishes standard forms, but their use is voluntary.

Q15 of 20 State Transactions

What is the property tax assessment ratio for residential property in Tennessee?

  1. A 55% of appraised value
  2. B 40% of appraised value
  3. C 100% of appraised value
  4. D 25% of appraised value
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D 25% of appraised value

Tennessee assesses residential and farm property at 25% of appraised value. Commercial/industrial is 40%, and public utility is 55%. The tax rate is then applied to this assessed value.

  4. D No violation if the listing is accurate

A 'blind ad' omits the firm name, which is prohibited in Tennessee. All ads must identify the brokerage firm, even when the affiliate broker's name also appears.

About the exam

More about the Tennessee real estate exam

The Tennessee real estate affiliate broker exam trips up plenty of first-time candidates. The Tennessee Real Estate Commission (TREC) scores the national and Tennessee state portions separately, so a strong national score won't rescue a weak state score. That gap on the state section is why Tennessee-specific preparation matters more than another round of national review.

The Tennessee state portion leans on the Tennessee Real Estate Broker Act of 1973, the designated agent and facilitator brokerage relationships that Tennessee defines by statute, the Tennessee residential property condition disclosure, the Real Estate Education and Recovery Account, the errors and omissions insurance every licensee must carry, and the trust and escrow rules TREC enforces. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need Tennessee-specific practice questions.

FAQ

Tennessee real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Tennessee real estate exam?

The Tennessee affiliate broker exam has 120 scored questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question Tennessee state portion. PSI also mixes in 5 to 10 unscored experimental questions that don't count toward your result. You get 240 minutes in total, 160 for the national portion and 80 for the state portion.

What's the passing score for the Tennessee real estate exam?

You need 70 percent on each portion, and the two are scored separately. That works out to 56 of 80 correct on the national portion and 28 of 40 on the state portion. You have to pass both to get licensed, so a high score on one portion doesn't make up for falling short on the other.

How much does the Tennessee real estate exam cost?

PSI charges $63 to sit both portions of the affiliate broker exam. If you only need to retake a single portion, it's $37 for the national portion or $26 for the state portion. After you pass, Tennessee's initial affiliate broker license fee is $91, and affiliate broker is the entry-level license the state issues.

How long is the Tennessee real estate exam?

240 minutes in total. PSI gives you 160 minutes for the 80-question national portion and 80 minutes for the 40-question state portion, plus up to 15 minutes for an introductory tutorial that doesn't count against your testing time. Each portion is timed and scored on its own, and your result appears on screen as soon as you finish.

Who administers the Tennessee real estate exam?

The Tennessee Real Estate Affiliate Broker Examination is administered by PSI for the Tennessee Real Estate Commission (TREC). You register and schedule your exam date through PSI.

Is the Tennessee real estate exam hard?

Plenty of first-time candidates pass, but the exam earns its reputation, and the state portion is where people stumble. The national portion is manageable for most candidates who actually studied. The state portion catches people off guard, because Tennessee's brokerage-relationship rules, disclosure requirements, and the way the Real Estate Commission is structured don't match what national prep material teaches. Honest practice on Tennessee-specific questions is usually the difference between passing the first time and paying to retake a portion.

What's on the Tennessee real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers general real estate principles like agency, contracts, financing, property ownership, valuation, and fair housing. The 40-question Tennessee state portion focuses on the duties and powers of the Real Estate Commission, Tennessee licensing and advertising rules, broker and affiliate relationships, the handling of documents and trust and escrow funds, agency and disclosure issues including designated agents and facilitators, and special areas like timeshare and property management. The state-specific material carries more weight than its share of questions suggests, since it's the part most candidates have never seen before.
