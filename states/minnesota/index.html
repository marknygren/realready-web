Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under Minnesota Statute §82.55, which term describes a person authorized to close real estate transactions on behalf of others?

  1. A Title representative
  2. B Closing agent
  3. C Escrow coordinator
  4. D Settlement officer
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Closing agent

Minnesota Statute §82.55 specifically defines a closing agent as a person authorized to handle real estate closings on behalf of others. This is a distinct statutory term in Minnesota law, separate from general industry terminology like escrow coordinator or settlement officer.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Where must a Minnesota broker maintain a trust account for client funds?

  1. A At whichever bank the salesperson who collected the funds chooses
  2. B In the broker's general business account, tracked by a separate ledger
  3. C Only at a financial institution physically located in Minnesota
  4. D In a separate trust account at a bank, savings association, or credit union, which need not be in Minnesota
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D In a separate trust account at a bank, savings association, or credit union, which need not be in Minnesota

Minnesota Statutes 82.75 require client money to be held in a separate trust account, never commingled with the broker's own funds. The depository may be a Minnesota bank or trust company, a savings association, a credit union, or even an out-of-state (foreign) bank that authorizes the commissioner to examine its records, so it does not have to be located in Minnesota. Brokers hold these funds in a pooled interest-bearing trust account, with the interest remitted to the Minnesota Housing Finance Agency.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which official heads the agency that regulates real estate licensees in Minnesota?

  1. A The Commissioner of Commerce
  2. B The Attorney General
  3. C The Real Estate Commission chair
  4. D The Governor
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A The Commissioner of Commerce

Minnesota does not have a separate real estate commission. The Commissioner of Commerce heads the Department of Commerce, which is the sole regulatory authority over real estate licensees. This is a key structural difference from many other states that use a dedicated real estate commission.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What is the primary purpose of the Minnesota Real Estate Recovery Fund?

  1. A To reimburse licensees for uncollected commissions
  2. B To fund broker continuing education programs
  3. C To provide loans for first-time homebuyers
  4. D To compensate consumers harmed by licensee fraud
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D To compensate consumers harmed by licensee fraud

The Minnesota Recovery Fund compensates consumers who suffer financial losses due to licensee fraud, deception, or dishonesty. It does not fund education, provide homebuyer loans, or reimburse licensees.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

A licensee represents the seller but discovers a major foundation crack during a showing. What duty applies to the buyer?

  1. A Disclose the material fact to the buyer
  2. B Refer the buyer to a home inspector
  3. C No duty since the buyer is not a client
  4. D Only disclose if the buyer asks directly
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Disclose the material fact to the buyer

Minnesota licensees must deal honestly and in good faith with ALL parties, not just their clients. This includes disclosing known material facts to the buyer even when representing the seller. Waiting to be asked or simply referring to an inspector does not satisfy this obligation.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

When must a Minnesota licensee provide a written agency disclosure to a consumer?

  1. A Within 48 hours of showing a property
  2. B At the time an offer is submitted
  3. C At first substantive contact
  4. D At the closing table before signing
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C At first substantive contact

Minnesota law requires written agency disclosure at the first substantive contact with a consumer, before providing any real estate services. Waiting until an offer is made or closing occurs is too late to satisfy this requirement.

  4. D Industrial warehouse properties only

Q8 of 20 State Practice

A seller asks their listing agent what the standard commission rate is in Minnesota. The agent should respond:

  1. A The state sets rates based on property value
  2. B Commissions are fully negotiable
  3. C The standard rate is 6% of sale price
  4. D The MLS determines rates for all listings
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Commissions are fully negotiable

Minnesota law prohibits any suggestion of a standard commission rate. All real estate commissions are freely negotiable between the broker and client. No agency, MLS, or trade organization may set or suggest fixed rates.

  3. C Sexual orientation and gender identity

Q10 of 20 State Practice

Under Minnesota law, a residential lease must be in writing when the term exceeds:

  1. A Three months
  2. B One year
  3. C Six months
  4. D Two years

Minnesota's statute of frauds requires leases with terms exceeding one year to be in writing. Shorter leases may be oral, though written agreements are recommended for all tenancies.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

A married couple in Minnesota owns a home titled solely in the husband's name. The husband wants to sell. Who must sign the deed?

  1. A Neither spouse if a power of attorney exists
  2. B Both spouses must sign the deed
  3. C Only the spouse with higher income
  4. D Only the husband since he holds title
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Both spouses must sign the deed

Minnesota homestead law requires both spouses to sign the deed for homestead property, even when title is held in only one spouse's name. This protects the non-titled spouse's homestead interest in the property.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

A developer creates a planned community in Minnesota in 2024. Which statute governs this development?

  1. A Chapter 500, the Uniform Planned Community Act
  2. B Chapter 515, original condominium statute
  3. C Chapter 515B, the MN CIO Act
  4. D Chapter 515A, the Uniform Condo Act
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Chapter 515B, the MN CIO Act

Chapter 515B, the Minnesota Common Interest Ownership Act, governs condos, co-ops, townhomes, and planned communities created after June 1, 1994. A 2024 planned community falls under this statute.

Minnesota uses a dual recording system. What are the two systems?

  2. B Abstract (regular) and Torrens
  3. C County recorder and state registrar
  4. D Grantor-grantee and tract index

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A real estate professional in Minnesota holds a license but works under a broker's supervision. Under §82.55, this person is classified as a:

  1. A Associate broker
  2. B Salesperson
  3. C Broker intern
  4. D Licensed assistant
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Salesperson

Under Minnesota's statutory definitions in §82.55, a salesperson is a licensee who practices real estate under the direct supervision of a licensed broker. Minnesota does not use the term 'associate broker' or 'broker intern' as a license classification.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker deposits $500 of personal funds into her trust account to cover monthly bank fees. Is this permissible under Minnesota law?

  1. A No, any personal funds constitute commingling
  2. B Yes, a small amount to keep the account open is allowed
  3. C Yes, up to $5,000 of personal funds is allowed
  4. D No, bank fees must be paid from a separate account
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Yes, a small amount to keep the account open is allowed

Minnesota law permits a broker to deposit a small amount of personal funds into the trust account solely to keep the account open and cover bank service charges. This narrow exception does not constitute prohibited commingling, provided the amount is minimal and used only for account maintenance.

Which duty applies to Minnesota licensees when dealing with ALL parties in a transaction?

  1. A Deal honestly and in good faith
  2. B Share all information between parties
  3. C Provide confidential negotiating advice
  4. D Advocate for the best possible price

Correct: A Deal honestly and in good faith

Minnesota law requires licensees to deal honestly and in good faith with all parties, regardless of whom they represent. Confidential advice and advocacy are fiduciary duties owed only to clients, and sharing all information between parties would violate confidentiality.

About the exam

More about the Minnesota real estate exam

Minnesota scores the national and 40-question state portions separately, so a strong national score won't save you if your grasp of Minnesota law is thin. The Minnesota Department of Commerce licenses agents, and that makes Minnesota-specific preparation just as important as the national material.

Minnesota tests its own rules in ways a national course never covers, from the license law in Chapter 82 that the Department of Commerce enforces, to the statutory agency-relationships disclosure and Minnesota's facilitator option that lets a licensee work a deal without representing either party, the seller's property disclosure and well disclosure required before closing, the Minnesota Common Interest Ownership Act that governs condominiums and townhomes, and the broker trust-account rules for earnest money. FAQ

Minnesota real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Minnesota real estate exam?

The Minnesota Real Estate Salesperson Examination has 120 scored multiple-choice questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question Minnesota state-law portion. PSI delivers both portions back-to-back in one computer-based session and mixes in a small number of unscored pretest questions that look identical to the scored ones, so the time spent on those does not count against you.

What's the passing score for the Minnesota real estate exam?

You need at least 75 percent on each portion, scored separately. That works out to 60 of 80 on the national portion and 30 of 40 on the state portion. If you pass one portion and fail the other, you only retake the portion you failed, and the single-portion retake costs less than sitting the whole exam again.

How much does the Minnesota real estate exam cost?

PSI charges $63 to sit the salesperson exam, and a single failed portion can be retaken for $39. After you pass, the initial salesperson license fee is $70 through the Minnesota Department of Commerce. You pay the exam fee to PSI when you schedule, and there is no limit on how many times you can retake.

How long is the Minnesota real estate exam?

Four hours total. PSI allots two and a half hours for the 80-question national portion and ninety minutes for the 40-question Minnesota portion, taken back-to-back in one sitting. PSI runs Minnesota test centers in St. Paul, Woodbury, Eagan, Blaine, Rochester, Mankato, Willmar, Brainerd, and Duluth, and your score report prints before you leave.

Who administers the Minnesota real estate exam?

The Minnesota Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered by PSI for the Minnesota Department of Commerce. You register and schedule your exam date through PSI.

Is the Minnesota real estate exam hard?

It's a serious test, and the part that trips people up is the 40-question Minnesota portion. The national half covers the same principles taught in every state, but the state questions reward candidates who actually studied Minnesota's license law, the agency-relationships disclosure and facilitator option, the seller's property and well disclosures, and the Common Interest Ownership Act. Most people who fail leaned too heavily on national material and underprepared on Minnesota law. Honest reps on Minnesota-specific practice questions are the difference between a first-time pass and a $63 retake.

What's on the Minnesota real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers property ownership, land-use controls, valuation and market analysis, financing, general principles of agency, mandated disclosures, contracts, transfer of title, the practice of real estate, real estate calculations, and a small specialty-areas group, in proportions set by PSI's content outline. The 40-question Minnesota portion covers four areas, real estate brokerage license law, interests in real property, conveyance procedures and protection of the parties, and financial instruments with their obligations, rights, and remedies. Minnesota-specific law runs through that whole state portion, especially Chapter 82 license law, the agency-relationships disclosure, the seller's property and well disclosures, and the Common Interest Ownership Act.

What's the best way to prepare for the Minnesota real estate exam?

After you finish Minnesota's 90-hour salesperson prelicense education, the three thirty-hour courses known as Course I, Course II, and Course III, the highest-leverage move is grinding Minnesota-specific practice questions with honest feedback on every miss. National principles you can pick up from any course, but the 40-question state portion rewards repetition on Minnesota agency rules, the required disclosures, and Chapter 82 license law.
