Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

In New York, which license category allows a person to operate their own real estate firm and supervise agents?

  1. A Real estate salesperson
  2. B Real estate broker
  3. C Associate broker
  4. D Licensed real estate assistant
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Real estate broker

Only a licensed real estate broker may operate a firm and supervise salespersons and associate brokers. Associate brokers hold a broker license but work under another broker rather than running their own office.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A salesperson leaves Broker A to join Broker B. Who is responsible for notifying the DOS of the termination?

  1. A No notification is required if done within 30 days
  2. B Broker A must notify the DOS
  3. C Broker B files on behalf of both parties
  4. D The salesperson files the termination notice
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Broker A must notify the DOS

When a salesperson leaves a brokerage, the terminating broker (Broker A) is responsible for notifying the DOS. The new broker (Broker B) then files the change of association to activate the salesperson under their supervision.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which New York state law provides broader protected classes than federal fair housing law?

  1. A Civil Practice Law and Rules Article 78
  2. B Real Property Law Article 12-A (Licensing)
  3. C General Business Law Article 25-A (Consumer)
  4. D Executive Law Article 15 (Human Rights Law)
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Executive Law Article 15 (Human Rights Law)

The New York Human Rights Law, codified in Executive Law Article 15, adds protected classes beyond federal fair housing protections, including age, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, military status, and source of income.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What must every real estate advertisement in New York include?

  1. A The salesperson's personal cell phone number
  2. B The name of the licensed broker
  3. C The MLS listing number and property address
  4. D The property's most recent assessed tax value
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The name of the licensed broker

New York law prohibits blind ads. Every real estate advertisement must identify the licensed broker responsible for the ad, regardless of the medium used.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

Under RPL Section 443, when must an agent provide the agency disclosure form to a consumer?

  1. A At first substantive contact
  2. B Within 48 hours of the first showing
  3. C When the purchase contract is signed
  4. D At the property closing
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A At first substantive contact

RPL Section 443 requires the agency disclosure form to be provided at first substantive contact, which is the first meaningful discussion about real estate needs, finances, or specific properties. Casual conversation does not trigger this requirement.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

Is dual agency legal in New York?

  1. A Only in commercial transactions valued over $1 million
  2. B No, it is strictly prohibited under all circumstances in NY
  3. C Yes, with advance informed consent from all parties
  4. D Yes, without any special disclosure or written consent required
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Yes, with advance informed consent from all parties

Dual agency is legal in New York but requires advance informed consent from all parties to the transaction. The broker must explain the risks and limitations of dual agency before proceeding.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

A seller of a three-unit residential property in New York asks the listing agent to complete the Property Condition Disclosure Statement on the seller's behalf. How should the agent respond?

  1. A Offer the $500 credit option instead
  2. B Refuse because agents must not fill out the PCDS
  3. C Fill it out but note the agent authored it
  4. D Complete it as long as the seller reviews and signs off afterward
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Refuse because agents must not fill out the PCDS

Under RPL Article 14, the Property Condition Disclosure Statement must be completed by the seller. An agent who fills it out risks liability and violates the statute. The $500 credit alternative was eliminated as of March 20, 2024, so the PCDS is now mandatory.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

A homeowner asks a licensed real estate salesperson to provide an appraisal of their property. How should the salesperson respond?

  1. A Offer a CMA but explain only appraisers may appraise
  2. B Refer the owner to the local MLS for automated valuation
  3. C Provide the appraisal if they have five years of experience
  4. D Complete it and label it a broker price opinion
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Offer a CMA but explain only appraisers may appraise

Licensed salespersons and brokers may prepare a comparative market analysis to help determine a listing price, but they must never call it an appraisal. Only a licensed or certified appraiser may perform an appraisal. Using the term 'appraisal' for a CMA is a violation of licensing law.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

Under New York law, which body has the authority to enact local zoning ordinances?

  1. A The zoning board of appeals
  2. B The municipality's legislative body
  3. C The county planning board
  4. D The state legislature through direct enactment
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The municipality's legislative body

New York delegates zoning power to municipalities through three parallel enabling statutes. Towns act under Town Law Article 16, villages under Village Law Article 7, and cities under General City Law Article 2-A. The local legislative body, such as a town board or city council, adopts and amends zoning regulations, while the ZBA interprets and grants relief from zoning but does not create the ordinances.

Q10 of 20 State Practice

A home inspector notes that the house has aluminum wiring throughout. Why is this a concern for buyers?

  1. A It is illegal in all New York municipalities
  2. B Aluminum wiring cannot handle any modern appliance loads
  3. C Insurance companies refuse to cover aluminum wiring
  4. D It creates a higher fire risk at connections
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D It creates a higher fire risk at connections

Aluminum wiring, common in homes built during the 1960s and 1970s, expands and contracts more than copper, creating loose connections that can overheat and cause fires. While not illegal, it requires special connectors and is a material fact that should be disclosed to buyers.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

New York is commonly referred to as an 'attorney state' for real estate transactions. What does this mean in practice?

  1. A Attorneys must be present at all property showings
  2. B Brokers are prohibited from drafting any documents
  3. C Attorneys typically prepare and review contracts of sale
  4. D Only licensed attorneys may sell real estate
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Attorneys typically prepare and review contracts of sale

New York is known as an 'attorney state' because attorneys play a central role in residential transactions. The seller's attorney typically prepares the contract of sale, and both buyer and seller customarily retain their own attorneys, although it is not legally required.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

Which type of deed provides the GREATEST protection to the buyer in a New York real estate transaction?

  1. A Full Covenant and Warranty deed
  2. B Bargain and Sale deed with covenants
  3. C Executor's deed from a fiduciary
  4. D Quitclaim deed with no warranties
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Full Covenant and Warranty deed

A Full Covenant and Warranty deed provides the most protection because it includes all five covenants: seizin, against encumbrances, quiet enjoyment, further assurance, and warranty. This gives the buyer the strongest guarantees of clear title and the grantor's obligation to defend it.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

New York follows which theory of mortgage law?

  1. A Equity theory
  2. B Intermediate theory
  3. C Title theory
  4. D Lien theory
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Lien theory

New York is a lien theory state, meaning the mortgage creates a lien against the property but the borrower retains title. The lender does not hold title to the property; instead, the mortgage serves as security for the loan.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

What is the New York State transfer tax rate on a residential property sold for $2,000,000?

  1. A 0.2% of the sale price
  2. B 1% of the sale price
  3. C 0.65% of the sale price
  4. D 0.4% of the sale price
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D 0.4% of the sale price

The New York State transfer tax rate is 0.4% for properties selling for under $3 million and 0.65% for properties at $3 million or above. At $2,000,000, the property falls below the $3M threshold, so the 0.4% rate applies, resulting in a tax of $8,000.

Q15 of 20 State Transactions

Which level of government does NOT impose a real property tax in New York?

  1. A County government agencies
  2. B New York State government
  3. C School district
  4. D City or town government
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B New York State government

New York State does not impose a state-level real property tax. Property taxes are levied exclusively by local jurisdictions including cities, towns, villages, counties, and school districts. This is a frequently tested distinction on the NY exam.

Q16 of 20 State Transactions

A buyer purchases a condominium unit in New York City. What type of property interest does the buyer receive?

  1. A A proprietary lease and stock certificate
  2. B Real property with a deed and individual title
  3. C Shares of stock in a corporation
  4. D A membership interest in a limited partnership
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Real property with a deed and individual title

Condominium ownership is real property. The buyer receives a deed conveying title to the individual unit plus an undivided interest in the common elements. Each unit has its own tax lot, and the owner pays property taxes directly.

Q17 of 20 State Transactions

Which agency sets annual rent increases for rent-stabilized apartments in New York City?

  1. A The New York State Legislature
  2. B DHCR (Division of Housing and Community Renewal)
  3. C The NYC Department of Buildings
  4. D The Rent Guidelines Board
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The Rent Guidelines Board

The Rent Guidelines Board (RGB) sets annual allowable rent increases for rent-stabilized apartments in New York City. The RGB considers factors such as operating costs, inflation, and housing market conditions when determining guidelines for one-year and two-year lease renewals.

Q18 of 20 State Transactions

A co-op shareholder pays $2,400 per month in maintenance. The co-op's accountant reports that 40% is attributable to the building's property taxes and underlying mortgage interest. How much of the annual maintenance may be tax-deductible?

  1. A $11,520
  2. B $28,800
  3. C $2,400
  4. D $17,280
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A $11,520

Co-op maintenance includes the building's property taxes, underlying mortgage interest, and operating expenses. Only the portions attributable to property taxes and mortgage interest are potentially deductible. Here: $2,400 x 12 = $28,800 annual maintenance x 40% = $11,520 deductible portion, subject to applicable tax limits.

Q19 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker opens a branch office 40 miles from the main office and assigns a salesperson to manage it. What does DOS Regulation 175.21 require?

  1. A An associate broker must manage the branch office
  2. B The broker must provide adequate supervision
  3. C The branch must be within 25 miles of the main office
  4. D A separate broker license is required for each branch
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The broker must provide adequate supervision

DOS Regulation 175.21 requires brokers to provide adequate supervision of all offices and licensees. There is no distance restriction, but the broker remains vicariously liable for the acts of supervised agents at every location.

Q20 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Can a New York salesperson be actively associated with two brokers simultaneously?

  1. A Only if the salesperson holds an associate broker license
  2. B No, dual employment is prohibited
  3. C Yes, if the brokers operate in different counties
  4. D Yes, with written consent from both brokers
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B No, dual employment is prohibited

New York prohibits dual employment for salespersons. A licensee may only be associated with one broker at a time. To change brokers, the current association must be terminated before a new one can be filed.

About the exam

More about the New York real estate exam

New York runs a single combined exam with no separate national and state portions, so national principles and New York law sit side by side on the same test. Every item is drawn from the 77-hour qualifying curriculum, and that blend is exactly why studying generic material alone leaves most first-timers short.

New York's Article 12-A license law, the mandatory agency disclosure form, the Property Condition Disclosure Act, rent regulation tightened by the 2019 Housing Stability and Tenant Protection Act, co-op and condo board approvals, and the Department of State's disciplinary powers all show up on the exam. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need New York-specific practice questions.

FAQ

New York real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the New York real estate exam?

The New York State Real Estate Salesperson Examination has 75 multiple-choice questions. The Department of State administers it as a single combined test, so there is no separate national portion. You have 90 minutes to finish.

What's the passing score for the New York real estate exam?

You need 70% to pass, which is 53 of the 75 questions correct. The Department of State reports your result as simply passed or failed with no numerical score, so every question counts.

How much does the New York real estate exam cost?

The state examination fee is $15 per attempt, the lowest in the country, and you schedule each sitting through your eAccessNY account. If you pass, the salesperson license fee is $65, and a passed exam result stays valid for two years while you find a sponsoring broker.

How long is the New York real estate exam?

Ninety minutes for all 75 questions. The Department of State runs the test at exam sites across the state, and because the result is pass or fail you'll want to keep a steady pace rather than agonize over any single item.

Who administers the New York real estate exam?

The New York State Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered directly by the New York Department of State, Division of Licensing Services. You register and schedule your exam date through the state's eAccessNY system.

Is the New York real estate exam hard?

The first-time pass rate sits around 51%, so roughly half of candidates walk out needing a retake. The questions aren't tricky for the sake of being tricky. They test whether you actually understand New York real estate law and practice. Most people who fail underestimated how much New York-specific material is on the test (Article 12-A license law, agency disclosure, rent regulation, co-op and condo rules) and leaned too hard on national principles. Honest practice on a New York-specific practice test is what separates a first-try pass from paying the $15 fee again.

What's on the New York real estate exam?

The 75-question test is built from the 77-hour qualifying curriculum and blends national principles with New York law. Expect license law and regulation under Article 12-A, the law of agency and disclosure, estates and interests, liens and easements, deeds and conveyances, title closing and costs, contracts of sale and leases, real estate finance, land use regulation, construction and environmental issues, and the valuation process. New York-specific material runs through the whole thing, especially agency disclosure, the Property Condition Disclosure Act, rent regulation, and the Department of State's enforcement role.
