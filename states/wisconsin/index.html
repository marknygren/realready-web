Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A newly licensed salesperson needs to update their contact information on file. Which entity would process this change?

  1. A DATCP
  2. B REEB
  3. C Equal Rights Division
  4. D DSPS
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D DSPS

DSPS handles administrative functions like record maintenance, application processing, and fee collection. While REEB sets policy and handles discipline, day-to-day administrative tasks are managed by DSPS.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A licensee deposits a client's earnest money into their personal checking account. Which ground for discipline does this represent?

  1. A Making false promises to a client
  2. B Acting as an undisclosed principal
  3. C Commingling client funds with personal funds
  4. D Failing to provide document copies
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Commingling client funds with personal funds

Commingling is mixing client trust funds with personal funds. Under §452.14, this is a specific ground for disciplinary action. Trust funds must be deposited into the firm's trust account, never a personal account.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which protected class is covered by Wisconsin's Open Housing Law but NOT by the federal Fair Housing Act?

  1. A Race
  2. B Familial status
  3. C Lawful source of income
  4. D National origin
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Lawful source of income

Wisconsin's Open Housing Law adds several protected classes beyond federal law, including lawful source of income (Section 8 vouchers, Social Security), sexual orientation, marital status, age, ancestry, and domestic abuse victim status.

Q4 of 20 State Practice

A Wisconsin licensee represents the seller exclusively. What type of agency is this?

  1. A Seller's agent
  2. B Dual agent
  3. C Transaction broker
  4. D Subagent
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Seller's agent

A seller's agent represents only the seller's interests in a transaction. Wisconsin recognizes four agency types: seller's agent, buyer's agent, dual agent, and subagent.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

What is the WB-1 form used for in Wisconsin?

  1. A Counter-Offer to Purchase
  2. B Buyer Agency Agreement
  3. C Residential Listing Contract
  4. D Residential Offer to Purchase
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Residential Listing Contract

The WB-1 is the REEB-approved Residential Listing Contract — Exclusive Right to Sell. It establishes the listing agreement between the seller and the listing firm.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

Who is ultimately responsible for ensuring a firm's licensees comply with Chapter 452?

  1. A The firm's office manager
  2. B Each individual salesperson
  3. C The designated broker
  4. D The DSPS licensing coordinator
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C The designated broker

Every Wisconsin real estate firm must have a designated broker who is responsible for supervising all licensees. The designated broker must ensure compliance with Chapter 452 and REEB rules.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

A broker receives earnest money on a Thursday afternoon. By when must it be deposited?

  1. A Within 72 hours (Sunday)
  2. B By the next business day (Friday)
  3. C Within 5 business days
  4. D By Saturday (within 48 hours)
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D By Saturday (within 48 hours)

Wisconsin requires all trust funds to be deposited within 48 hours of receipt. If received before a holiday, 2 business days are allowed. In this case, the 48-hour deadline is Saturday.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

Under Chapter 709, within how many days of acceptance must the seller provide the Real Estate Condition Report?

  1. A 10 days
  2. B 5 days
  3. C 30 days
  4. D 15 days
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A 10 days

Wisconsin law requires sellers to furnish a completed Real Estate Condition Report to prospective buyers no later than 10 days after acceptance of the contract.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

What must every real estate advertisement in Wisconsin include?

  1. A The REEB approval stamp
  2. B The firm name as licensed with DSPS
  3. C The MLS listing number
  4. D The salesperson's license number
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The firm name as licensed with DSPS

All real estate advertising in Wisconsin must include the firm name as it is licensed with DSPS. The salesperson's name may also appear, but the firm name is mandatory.

Q10 of 20 State Transactions

The WB-11 is Wisconsin's primary form for which type of transaction?

  1. A Residential Listing Contract
  2. B Commercial Offer to Purchase
  3. C Vacant Land Offer to Purchase
  4. D Residential Offer to Purchase
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Residential Offer to Purchase

The WB-11 is the Residential Offer to Purchase — Wisconsin's primary transaction document for residential real estate. It becomes binding upon acceptance.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

Under Wis. Admin. Code REEB 16.04, what must Wisconsin licensees use for real estate transactions?

  1. A REEB-approved forms where they exist
  2. B Any standardized real estate forms
  3. C Forms from the Wisconsin REALTORS Association
  4. D Forms created by their brokerage firm
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A REEB-approved forms where they exist

Wis. Admin. Code REEB 16.04 requires licensees to use board-approved forms (the WB series) when acting as an agent or party in a real estate transaction, except where no approved form exists. Licensees cannot create their own forms or use forms from other sources.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

Two siblings inherit a property. Without any agreement specifying otherwise, how do they hold title?

  1. A In sole ownership by the eldest
  2. B As community property
  3. C As joint tenants
  4. D As tenants in common
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D As tenants in common

In Wisconsin, when multiple owners take title without specifying the type of ownership, the default is tenancy in common. Joint tenancy requires specific language and the four unities.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

Which deed type provides the greatest protection for the buyer in Wisconsin?

  1. A Quitclaim deed
  2. B Special warranty deed
  3. C Warranty deed
  4. D Personal representative's deed
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Warranty deed

A warranty deed provides the most protection, as the grantor warrants title against all defects, both past and present. It is the most common deed type used in Wisconsin residential transactions.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

What is Wisconsin's real estate transfer fee rate?

  1. A $1.00 per $100 of value
  2. B $0.30 per $100 of value
  3. C $0.10 per $100 of value
  4. D $0.50 per $100 of value
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B $0.30 per $100 of value

Wisconsin imposes a real estate transfer fee of $0.30 per $100 of value (or fraction thereof) on every conveyance of real property not otherwise exempt.

Q15 of 20 State Transactions

Which agency administers ATCP 134, Wisconsin's residential rental practices rule?

  1. A Real Estate Examining Board
  2. B Department of Safety and Professional Services
  3. C Department of Workforce Development
  4. D Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection

ATCP 134 is administered by the Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection (DATCP). It governs residential rental agreements, disclosures, security deposits, and prohibited landlord practices.

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What is the relationship between DSPS and the Real Estate Examining Board?

  1. A DSPS reports to REEB on licensing matters
  2. B They are separate and independent agencies
  3. C REEB operates as a regulatory board within DSPS
  4. D REEB is a federal agency that oversees DSPS
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C REEB operates as a regulatory board within DSPS

DSPS is the umbrella state agency providing administrative support, while the REEB operates within DSPS as the regulatory body that makes substantive decisions about licensure, forms, education, and discipline.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A salesperson tells a buyer the roof was replaced last year, knowing it was not. This is grounds for discipline as:

  1. A Acting as an undisclosed principal
  2. B Making false promises or misrepresentations
  3. C Failing to account for funds
  4. D Incompetence in practice
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Making false promises or misrepresentations

Knowingly making false statements about a property is a misrepresentation under §452.14. This is one of the most common grounds for disciplinary action against licensees.

Q18 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A landlord refuses to rent to a tenant because they plan to pay with Section 8 vouchers. Under Wisconsin law, this is:

  1. A Legal unless the property has 5+ units
  2. B Illegal discrimination based on lawful source of income
  3. C Illegal only in Milwaukee and Madison
  4. D Legal because landlords can choose tenants
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Illegal discrimination based on lawful source of income

Wisconsin's Open Housing Law protects 'lawful source of income' as a protected class, which includes Section 8 vouchers, Social Security, and other government assistance. Refusing to rent based on this is illegal statewide.

Q19 of 20 State Practice

A licensee wants to represent both the buyer and seller in the same transaction. What is required?

  1. A Only the broker's approval is needed
  2. B Informed written consent from both parties
  3. C Dual agency is prohibited in Wisconsin
  4. D Verbal agreement from both parties
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Informed written consent from both parties

Wisconsin allows dual agency but requires informed written consent from both the buyer and seller. Both parties must understand the limitations of dual representation before agreeing.

Q20 of 20 State Practice

A seller asks their agent what the standard commission rate is. How should the agent respond?

  1. A Refer the seller to the REEB for guidance
  2. B Explain that there is no standard commission rate
  3. C State that commissions are set by the MLS
  4. D Quote the rate that most agents in the area charge
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Explain that there is no standard commission rate

Since 2024, all WB listing forms include the statement: 'There is no standard market commission rate.' Commission rates are always negotiable between the seller and the listing firm.

About the exam

More about the Wisconsin real estate exam

The Wisconsin real estate salesperson exam is a single combined test with no separate national and state sections, so Wisconsin's own license law shows up in questions across the whole exam. That means generic national study won't be enough, and Wisconsin-specific preparation is what gets candidates to a passing score.

Wisconsin tests its own WB approved forms like the WB-11 Residential Offer to Purchase, the powers of the Real Estate Examining Board, agency-disclosure rules, broker trust-account requirements, the real estate condition report sellers must provide, and Wisconsin fair housing protections. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need Wisconsin-specific practice questions.

FAQ

Wisconsin real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Wisconsin real estate exam?

The Wisconsin real estate salesperson exam has 140 scored multiple-choice questions, plus a handful of unscored pretest items mixed in that don't count toward your result. The Department of Safety and Professional Services gives it as one combined test through Pearson VUE, so there's no separate national portion to clear.

What's the passing score for the Wisconsin real estate exam?

You need a score of 75 to pass. Wisconsin reports your result as a scaled score on a 0 to 100 scale rather than a raw percentage, so a 75 reflects how your performance compares across different exam versions, not simply 75% of the questions correct. You find out whether you passed before you leave the test center.

How much does the Wisconsin real estate exam cost?

The examination fee is $65, paid to Pearson VUE when you reserve your seat. The fee is the same on every attempt, so if you have to retake the exam you pay $65 again. Payment is due at reservation and isn't accepted at the test center.

How long is the Wisconsin real estate exam?

You get four hours, which is 240 minutes, for the 140-question salesperson exam. That's generous compared with many states, so most candidates have time to read carefully and review the questions they flagged before the clock runs out.

Who administers the Wisconsin real estate exam?

The Wisconsin Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered by Pearson VUE for the Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services (DSPS). You register and schedule your exam date through Pearson VUE.

Is the Wisconsin real estate exam hard?

It's challenging, and the Wisconsin-specific material is where most people lose points. Candidates often handle national topics like financing and valuation well, then get tripped up by Wisconsin's approved forms, agency-disclosure rules, and trust-account requirements. Focused practice on Wisconsin law is what separates first-time passers from retakes.

What's on the Wisconsin real estate salesperson exam?

The 140-question exam blends national real estate principles with Wisconsin law across ten areas. Agency is the largest at about 32 questions, followed by contract law and Wisconsin's WB approved forms at about 22, valuing and financing property at about 20, ownership and transfer of property at about 14, business ethics at about 14, and disclosures at about 13. The rest is split among land use controls, fair housing, federal antitrust law, and a miscellaneous group that covers trust accounts, fee splitting, and rental practices.
