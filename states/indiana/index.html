Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

How are members of the Indiana Real Estate Commission appointed?

  1. A Elected by licensed brokers statewide
  2. B Appointed by the PLA director
  3. C Appointed by the Governor
  4. D Selected by the Indiana General Assembly
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Appointed by the Governor

IREC members are appointed by the Governor of Indiana, consistent with the structure of most Indiana professional licensing boards. This gubernatorial appointment process ensures public accountability for the commission's oversight role.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under Indiana's licensee-complaint process (IC 25-1-7), who may file a complaint against a licensed broker?

  1. A Any person with knowledge of a potential violation
  2. B Only the PLA on IREC's behalf
  3. C Only the buyer or seller in the transaction
  4. D Only licensed brokers or managing brokers
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Any person with knowledge of a potential violation

Any person may file. Complaints are written, signed, and filed with the director of the Attorney General's consumer protection division, which investigates; complaints with merit are submitted to IREC, and the Attorney General prosecutes before the Commission.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Under Indiana law, which of the following activities requires a real estate license?

  1. A Negotiating a lease on behalf of another for compensation
  2. B Selling your own home without using a broker
  3. C Referring a friend to a broker without receiving a fee
  4. D Managing rental property you personally own
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Negotiating a lease on behalf of another for compensation

Negotiating leases for others for compensation is a licensed activity in Indiana. Owners selling their own property and people managing property they own are exempt. Referring someone without receiving compensation does not trigger licensing requirements.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

An Indiana broker is licensed but not currently affiliated with a managing broker. What status best describes her license?

  1. A Unassigned status
  2. B Inactive status
  3. C Suspended status
  4. D Referral status
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Unassigned status

An Indiana broker who holds a current license but is not affiliated with a broker company is in unassigned status. Inactive status means the broker has affirmatively chosen not to practice. Referral status is a separate category allowing limited referral activity. Unassigned and inactive are distinct classifications.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

A seller's broker in Indiana owes which fiduciary duty that requires placing the client's interests above all others?

  1. A Fairness
  2. B Loyalty
  3. C Disclosure
  4. D Accountability
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Loyalty

Loyalty is the fiduciary duty that places the client's interests above the broker's own and all others; in Indiana it is expressed statutorily as the duty to promote the client's interests (IC 25-34.1-10-10(a)(3)). Disclosure and accounting are also duties owed to the client, but they do not by themselves require elevating the client's interests above all others; fairness/honesty is the duty owed to all parties (IC 25-34.1-10-10(c)).

Q6 of 20 State Practice

Under Indiana law, the written agency disclosure a licensee must give a consumer at the beginning of an agency relationship must identify which of the following?

  1. A The broker's commission rate
  2. B The type of agency relationship being offered
  3. C A list of the broker's recent sales
  4. D The broker's errors and omissions insurance carrier
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The type of agency relationship being offered

At the beginning of an agency relationship, a licensee must disclose in writing the broker company's written office policy, which identifies and describes the agency relationships a licensee may have — seller, landlord, buyer, or tenant agency — and whether the firm permits or rejects disclosed limited agency (IC 25-34.1-10-13(a)-(b)). Commission rates, sales history, and insurance carriers are not required elements; separately, parties must be advised whether compensation will be shared with other broker companies (IC 25-34.1-10-13(c)).

Q7 of 20 State Practice

Under Indiana professional standards, a broker's duty of competence requires which of the following?

  1. A Performing services with reasonable skill and care
  2. B Obtaining a specialist designation before assisting clients
  3. C Guaranteeing transaction outcomes for clients
  4. D Consulting an attorney on every real estate transaction
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Performing services with reasonable skill and care

Indiana's professional standard of competence requires brokers to perform services with the reasonable skill and care expected of a licensed professional. This does not require specialist designations on every transaction or attorney involvement in each deal. Brokers cannot guarantee outcomes but must exercise sound professional judgment.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

An Indiana broker discovers during a listing walkthrough that the home has an active termite infestation. The seller instructs the broker to say nothing. The broker must:

  1. A Withdraw from the listing rather than disclose
  2. B Follow the seller's instruction — the seller controls disclosure
  3. C Disclose the infestation to prospective buyers despite the seller's instruction
  4. D Disclose only if asked directly by a buyer
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Disclose the infestation to prospective buyers despite the seller's instruction

Under IC 25-34.1-10-10(d), Indiana brokers must disclose to prospective buyers adverse material facts actually known concerning the physical condition of the property, regardless of seller instructions — an active termite infestation is a classic latent defect. A seller cannot instruct a broker to withhold it; the statute's confidentiality duty expressly gives way where disclosure is required by law. While withdrawal is an option, the required act is disclosure.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

Under Indiana advertising rules, which element is required on all real estate advertisements?

  1. A The firm's IPLA registration number
  2. B The managing broker's personal name
  3. C The individual broker's license number
  4. D The brokerage firm's name
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The brokerage firm's name

Indiana requires the brokerage firm's name to appear on all real estate advertisements. An individual broker's personal name, license number, or the firm's IPLA number are not independently required. Ads that omit the firm name are considered blind ads and are prohibited.

Q10 of 20 State Transactions

A seller asks her broker to prepare a listing agreement. Under Indiana law, which form is required for the agreement to be enforceable?

  1. A Verbal agreement with written follow-up within 30 days
  2. B An implied agreement based on the broker's marketing efforts
  3. C Oral agreement confirmed by email
  4. D Written agreement signed by both parties
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Written agreement signed by both parties

Indiana requires listing agreements to be in writing with a definite expiration date (IC 25-34.1-12-1), and under the statute of frauds a commission contract is not valid unless it is in writing and signed by the owner (IC 32-21-1-10). In practice both parties sign the listing. An oral or implied agreement gives the broker no enforceable right to a commission.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

A listing broker receives an offer on her seller's property at 5:00 PM on a Friday. The seller is traveling. What is the broker's obligation?

  1. A Present only if the offer meets the seller's minimum price
  2. B Present the offer promptly, regardless of timing or inconvenience
  3. C Hold the offer until Monday when the seller returns to the office
  4. D Wait until a second offer arrives to present both simultaneously
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Present the offer promptly, regardless of timing or inconvenience

Indiana requires all offers to be presented promptly to the seller. A broker cannot delay presentation based on the seller's travel schedule, the offer price, or a hope that a better offer will arrive. Withholding or delaying an offer is a violation of the broker's fiduciary duty.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

Under IC 32-21-5, what standard must a seller meet when completing Indiana's Seller Disclosure form?

  1. A Disclosure of all defects whether known or discoverable
  2. B Disclosure only of defects identified by a licensed home inspector
  3. C Good faith disclosure of all known material defects
  4. D Certification that the property is free of all material defects
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Good faith disclosure of all known material defects

Indiana's Seller Disclosure Act (IC 32-21-5) requires sellers to disclose known material defects in good faith. Sellers are not required to investigate for unknown defects or guarantee the property's condition — only to honestly disclose what they actually know.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

How long must Indiana listing and selling brokers retain copies of closing statements after a transaction?

  1. A A minimum of seven years to match federal tax requirements
  2. B A minimum of five years
  3. C A minimum of three years
  4. D Until the transaction's first anniversary only
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B A minimum of five years

876 IAC 8-2-3 requires listing and selling brokers to retain copies of closing statements for at least five years. Listing agreements and trust account records must also be retained and are subject to IREC inspection, though no specific period is stated for them.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

A buyer's attorney is not present at closing in Indiana. Is the closing still legally valid?

  1. A Yes, attorney attendance at closing is optional in Indiana
  2. B Yes, but only for transactions under $500,000
  3. C No, Indiana requires an attorney to oversee all residential closings
  4. D No, an attorney must at least review all documents before closing
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Yes, attorney attendance at closing is optional in Indiana

Indiana is an attorney-optional state for real estate closings. Title companies typically conduct closings without attorney involvement, which is legally valid. Buyers and sellers may choose to hire attorneys, but it is not required by Indiana law.

Q15 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker believes an IREC rule exceeds the Commission's statutory authority. Which of the following best describes IREC's rulemaking power?

  1. A IREC may adopt rules necessary to implement real estate licensing statutes
  2. B IREC may create rules with no statutory basis if justified by public interest
  3. C IREC may only issue guidance documents, not binding rules
  4. D IREC rules require annual legislative approval to remain valid
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A IREC may adopt rules necessary to implement real estate licensing statutes

IREC has rulemaking authority to adopt rules necessary to carry out the real estate licensing statutes. Rules must be grounded in statutory authority; the Commission cannot create rules beyond its enabling legislation. A broker challenging a rule would need to show it exceeds that authority.

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

In an IREC disciplinary proceeding, who prosecutes the case and who decides it?

  1. A The complainant prosecutes; the IREC chairperson decides alone
  2. B The Attorney General's office prosecutes; the Commission itself hears the case and issues the final order
  3. C The PLA director prosecutes and an independent ALJ issues the final order
  4. D A circuit court judge hears the case and reports to IREC
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The Attorney General's office prosecutes; the Commission itself hears the case and issues the final order

Under IC 25-1-7-7 the Attorney General prosecutes disciplinary complaints before the Commission, and the Commission sits as the administrative law judge, issuing findings and a final order under the Administrative Orders and Procedures Act (IC 4-21.5). The board may designate a hearing officer, but the final order is the Commission's, reviewable in court within 30 days.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A licensed auctioneer is hired to auction a parcel of real property at a public auction. Does this person also need an Indiana real estate license?

  1. A Yes, all real property sales require a real estate license regardless of method
  2. B No, licensed auctioneers conducting public auctions are exempt under IC 25-34.1-3-2
  3. C Yes, unless the auction is conducted on the property itself
  4. D No, but only if the auctioneer does not advertise the property beforehand
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B No, licensed auctioneers conducting public auctions are exempt under IC 25-34.1-3-2

Indiana's licensing statute (IC 25-34.1-3-2) explicitly exempts licensed auctioneers conducting sales at public auction under IC 25-6.1. This is one of several statutory exemptions, alongside exemptions for attorneys (for acts constituting the practice of law), public officials performing acts authorized by law, and court-appointed fiduciaries.

Q18 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker on referral status in Indiana receives a request from a former client to show properties and write a contract. Can the broker do this?

  1. A No, the broker must first upgrade to unassigned status before showing property
  2. B No, referral status limits activity to referring clients to active brokers
  3. C Yes, but only for former clients with whom the broker has a prior relationship
  4. D Yes, referral status allows all licensed activities if unpaid
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B No, referral status limits activity to referring clients to active brokers

Referral status restricts the broker to making referrals only — showing property and writing contracts are full brokerage activities that require active affiliated status. The broker would need to change to active affiliated status before engaging in these activities. The restriction applies regardless of whether compensation is involved.

Q19 of 20 State Practice

A buyer's broker in Indiana negotiates a purchase price well below the seller's asking price, saving the buyer $15,000. This is an example of which fiduciary duty?

  1. A Disclosure — revealing seller motivation to the buyer
  2. B Loyalty — acting in the buyer's best financial interest
  3. C Obedience — following the buyer's exact instructions
  4. D Confidentiality — protecting buyer financial information
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Loyalty — acting in the buyer's best financial interest

Aggressively negotiating to save the buyer money is the duty of loyalty — placing the client's interests paramount. While obedience is also a fiduciary duty, it applies to following lawful instructions, not proactive advocacy. Confidentiality and disclosure are separate duties.

Q20 of 20 State Practice

A buyer calls an Indiana broker to inquire about a listed property. After briefly discussing the neighborhood, the broker begins asking about the buyer's budget and financing. At what point must the agency disclosure be made?

  1. A When the buyer submits a written offer
  2. B Before the discussion about budget and financing begins
  3. C At or before the signing of a buyer agency agreement
  4. D At the first in-person showing of the property
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Before the discussion about budget and financing begins

Indiana requires the written office-policy disclosure at the beginning of an agency relationship and before the potential client discloses any confidential information specific to them (IC 25-34.1-10-13(b)). A buyer's budget and financing are exactly that kind of confidential information, so disclosure must come before that discussion begins — waiting until a showing or an offer is too late.

About the exam

More about the Indiana real estate exam

Indiana scores its national and state-specific portions separately, so a strong national score won't carry you if your grasp of Indiana law is thin. Indiana also uses the word "broker" for what most other states call a salesperson license, so the entry-level exam covers full broker scope from day one. That makes Indiana-specific preparation matter as much as the national material.

Indiana tests its own rules in ways a national course never touches: the required written agency disclosure, the seller's residential real estate sales disclosure form, the Consumer Restitution (Recovery) Fund, trust-account and earnest-money rules, the psychologically affected properties statute, and the Indiana Real Estate Commission's enforcement powers under IC 25-34.1 and 876 IAC all show up on the exam. FAQ

Indiana real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Indiana real estate broker exam?

The Indiana Real Estate Broker Examination has 130 scored questions — 80 on the national portion and 50 on the state-specific portion. The exam also includes unscored pretest items (5 on the national portion and 5–10 on the state portion, for up to 145 total items) that look identical to scored questions, so you should answer everything.

What's the passing score for the Indiana broker exam?

Indiana uses a scaled score of 75 on each portion, set by the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency together with Pearson VUE. The scaled score isn't the same as a raw percentage. You must pass both the national and state portions individually. If you pass one and fail the other, you only need to retake the failed part.

How much does the Indiana real estate broker exam cost?

The Pearson VUE examination fee is $55 per attempt, paid by credit card, debit card, or voucher at the time you reserve your seat. The fee is non-refundable and non-transferable. After you pass, the broker license application fee paid to the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency is $60, and the license renews every three years for another $60.

How long is the Indiana real estate broker exam?

Total seat time is 240 minutes (four hours), which covers both the national and state portions; the pre-exam computer tutorial doesn't count against your exam time. Pearson VUE administers the exam in person at testing centers across the state.

Who administers the Indiana real estate exam?

The Indiana Real Estate Broker Examination is administered by Pearson VUE for the Indiana Real Estate Commission. You register and schedule your exam date through Pearson VUE.

Is the Indiana real estate broker exam hard?

Yes, more than most candidates expect. Indiana is one of a smaller group of states where the entry-level license is the broker license, and the exam tests full broker scope (agency relationships, trust accounts, brokerage agreements, supervision of unlicensed assistants) instead of just salesperson basics. The state portion is dense with statute and rule, and a single missed concept on something like the written agency disclosure or the seller's disclosure form can pull your scaled score below 75. Honest practice on Indiana-specific questions, not generic national content, is what gets people across the line on the first try.

What's on the Indiana real estate broker exam?

The 80-question national portion covers real property characteristics and land use, forms of ownership and transfer of title, property value and appraisal, real estate contracts and agency, real estate practice and fair housing, property disclosures and environmental issues, financing and settlement, and real estate math calculations. The 50-question state portion covers five areas: the Indiana Real Estate Commission's general powers and disciplinary authority (5 items); licensing categories and maintenance (9 items); statutory and regulatory requirements like advertising, commissions, brokerage agreements, earnest money, the seller's residential real estate sales disclosure, and psychologically affected properties (12 items); statutes and rules governing agency relationships and licensee conduct (17 items); and real estate office procedures including trust accounts, branch offices, and the managing broker relationship (7 items).
