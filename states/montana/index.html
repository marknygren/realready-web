Correct: C The Board of Realty Regulation

The Montana Board of Realty Regulation has authority to investigate complaints filed by consumers against licensees. The Board may examine broker records and take disciplinary action as warranted.

Which activity requires a Montana real estate license?

  1. A Negotiating a sale for compensation
  2. B Appraising property for a lender
  3. C Selling your own property
  4. D Giving free advice to a neighbor

Correct: A Negotiating a sale for compensation

Negotiating a real estate sale for compensation on behalf of another person requires a Montana real estate license. Selling one's own property, giving free advice, and appraising are not activities requiring a real estate license.

Under MCA 37-51-325, all active Montana real estate licensees are required to carry errors and omissions insurance. There are no exemptions based on license type or experience level.

Q10 of 20 State Practice

How long must a Montana broker retain transaction documents?

  1. A 3 years
  2. B 8 years
  3. C 5 years
  4. D 10 years
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B 8 years

Per ARM 24.210.601, Montana brokers must retain trust account records and real estate transaction documents (sales contracts, leases, options, agency agreements, closing statements) for 8 years from the later of the date of receipt or the date the transaction was completed. The 8-year period matches the statute of limitations on written contracts under MCA 27-2-202(1). Records may be paper or electronic and must be readily available for Board inspection.

About the exam

More about the Montana real estate exam

Montana scores its national and 40-question state-law portions separately, so a strong national score won't carry you if your grasp of Montana law is thin. That makes Montana-specific preparation matter as much as the national material.

Montana tests its own rules in ways a national course never touches, from the Title 37 Chapter 51 brokerage relationships law that lets a licensee act as a statutory broker without becoming the agent of either party, to the mandatory relationship disclosure required at the first reasonable opportunity, the Small Tract Financing Act trust indenture and 120-day non-judicial trustee's sale that replaces the ordinary mortgage foreclosure on tracts of 40 acres or less, Montana's prior-appropriation water rights and the way water rights pass with the land, the Real Estate Recovery Account that pays consumer judgments when a licensee can't, and an errors-and-omissions insurance mandate that every active licensee must satisfy every year. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies, so a generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need Montana-specific practice questions.

Montana real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Montana real estate exam?

The Montana Real Estate Salesperson Examination has 120 scored multiple-choice questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question Montana state-law portion. Pearson VUE delivers both portions in one computer-based session and adds five unscored pretest questions on the national portion and up to ten on the state portion. What's the passing score for the Montana real estate exam?

The two portions are scored separately on a scaled 0–100 scale, and you need a 75 on each. Scaling means your raw score is statistically adjusted for the difficulty of the specific form you took, so 75 scaled is not the same as 75 percent correct. If you pass one portion and fail the other you only retake the portion you failed, and your authorization to test stays valid for up to five years after you finished the first required pre-licensing course.

Is the Montana real estate exam hard?

It's a serious test, and the part that catches people off guard is the 40-question Montana portion. The national half covers the same principles taught in every state, but the state questions reward candidates who actually studied Montana's brokerage relationships law, the relationship disclosure form, the statutory broker concept, the Small Tract Financing Act, and Montana's errors-and-omissions insurance rules. Most people who fail leaned too heavily on national material and underprepared on Montana law. Honest reps on Montana-specific practice questions are the difference between a first-time pass and a $95 retake.

What's on the Montana real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers real property characteristics and legal descriptions, forms of ownership and transfer of title, property value and appraisal, real estate contracts and agency, the practice of real estate including fair housing, property disclosures and environmental issues, financing and settlement, and real estate math. Contracts and agency together carry the most weight, with sixteen items on the national outline. The 40-question Montana portion covers the Board of Realty Regulation's investigatory and disciplinary powers, licensing activities and license maintenance, the substantive requirements that govern the day-to-day activities of licensees including advertising, broker–salesperson supervision, unprofessional conduct, compensation, agency and property disclosure, handling documents and handling monies, and additional topics like errors-and-omissions insurance, common-interest ownership, the Residential Landlord and Tenant Act, and foreclosure and redemption. Activities of licensees is by far the heaviest state topic with 28 of the 40 items, so it is worth extra attention.
