Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A consumer believes a licensee committed fraud during a transaction. Where should they file a complaint?

  1. A The local Board of REALTORS
  2. B The Association of REALTORS
  3. C The county district court
  4. D The Division of Real Estate
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The Division of Real Estate

The Division of Real Estate investigates complaints against licensees, conducts audits, and initiates administrative proceedings. The Association of REALTORS is a private professional organization, not a government regulatory body.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which person is exempt from Utah real estate licensing requirements?

  1. A An unlicensed assistant showing property
  2. B An attorney acting within their practice
  3. C A part-time negotiator paid per deal
  4. D A property owner's friend helping for a fee
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B An attorney acting within their practice

Active attorneys acting within the scope of their legal practice are exempt from licensing. Other exemptions include persons acting under court order and government employees performing official duties.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What is RELMS in Utah real estate?

  1. A The real estate recovery fund database
  2. B The online license management system
  3. C An MLS alternative for rural areas
  4. D A standardized listing contract form
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The online license management system

RELMS (Real Estate License Management System) is Utah's online system for managing license-related activities including renewals, transfers, terminations, activation/inactivation, and continuing education tracking.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Who may file a complaint against a Utah real estate licensee with the Division?

  1. A Only parties to the transaction
  2. B Any person
  3. C Only licensed real estate professionals
  4. D Only the Real Estate Commission
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Any person

Anyone can file a complaint with the Division of Real Estate. The Division then investigates and may take action ranging from dismissal to formal administrative proceedings.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

Utah recognizes which types of brokerage agency relationships?

  1. A Seller, buyer, designated, and transaction
  2. B Seller, buyer, and limited agency
  3. C Single agency and dual agency only
  4. D Seller and buyer agency only
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Seller, buyer, and limited agency

Utah recognizes three types: seller agency, buyer agency, and limited agency (dual agency where the broker represents both parties with informed consent).

Correct: B A federally insured Utah institution

Principal brokers must maintain trust accounts at a federally insured financial institution located in Utah. This ensures the funds are protected by federal deposit insurance.

All advertising must include the brokerage name as registered with the Division. Advertising without brokerage identification is considered a blind ad and is prohibited regardless of the platform.

Q19 of 20 State Practice

A broker represents both the buyer and seller in the same transaction with the informed consent of both parties. This relationship is called:

  1. A Facilitator relationship
  2. B Designated agency
  3. C Limited agency
  4. D Transaction brokerage
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Limited agency

In Utah, when a broker represents both parties with informed consent, it is called limited agency. The broker owes limited duties to both parties and cannot advocate for one over the other.

About the exam

More about the Utah real estate exam

Utah's real estate exam carries a 50-question state section scored on its own, and it's where first-time candidates most often slip. A strong national score won't rescue a weak state score, so Utah-specific preparation matters more here than another pass through national review.

The Utah section leans on the Real Estate Licensing and Practice Act and Division rules, the limited agency relationship Utah uses in place of dual agency, the state-approved Real Estate Purchase Contract a sales agent fills in rather than drafts, trust account and earnest money handling, the Real Estate Education Research and Recovery Fund, and special Utah topics like prior appropriation water rights and the Lien Recovery Fund. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need Utah-specific practice questions.

FAQ

Utah real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Utah real estate exam?

The Utah Real Estate Sales Agent Examination has 130 scored questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 50-question Utah state portion. Pearson VUE also mixes in unscored pretest questions, 5 on the national portion and 5 to 10 on the state portion, that don't count toward your result. You get four hours in total to finish both portions.

What's the passing score for the Utah real estate exam?

Utah uses a scaled score that runs from 0 to 100, and you need a 70 on each of the two portions, the national and the Utah state portion, scored separately. The scaled score is not the raw count or percentage of questions you got right, it is an equated score that adjusts for small differences in difficulty between exam forms. Because the portions are scored separately, a strong score on one won't offset a low score on the other. If you pass one portion and fail the other, you only retake the failed portion within six months.

How much does the Utah real estate exam cost?

Pearson VUE charges $69 to sit the Utah sales agent exam, paid by card or voucher when you make your reservation. The fee is non-refundable and non-transferable, and you pay it again for each examination reservation, including a retake of a single portion. Sales agent is the entry-level real estate license Utah issues.

How long is the Utah real estate exam?

Four hours in total. Pearson VUE runs the national and Utah portions in a single four-hour session, and the exam ends automatically when your time expires. You report to the test center about 30 minutes early and leave with an official pass or fail score report in hand.

Who administers the Utah real estate exam?

The Utah Real Estate Sales Agent Examination is administered by Pearson VUE for the Utah Division of Real Estate. You register and schedule your exam date through Pearson VUE.

Is the Utah real estate exam hard?

Plenty of first-time candidates pass, but the exam earns its reputation and the Utah state portion is where people stumble. The 80-question national portion is manageable for anyone who actually studied general principles. The 50-question state portion catches people off guard, because Utah's limited agency model, the state-approved Real Estate Purchase Contract, trust account rules, and topics like prior appropriation water rights and the Lien Recovery Fund don't match what national prep material teaches. Honest practice on Utah-specific questions is usually the difference between passing the first time and paying to retake a portion.

What's on the Utah real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers general real estate principles, property characteristics and ownership, valuation and appraisal, contracts and agency, real estate practice, disclosures and environmental issues, financing and settlement, and real estate math. The 50-question Utah portion focuses on the Division of Real Estate and the Real Estate Commission, licensing and license maintenance, office procedures and trust accounts, licensee practice and approved forms, agency including limited agency, enforcement, the Real Estate Education Research and Recovery Fund, and special Utah topics like water rights, the Lien Recovery Fund, and closing statements. The Utah material carries more weight than its share of questions suggests, because it is the part most candidates have never seen before.
