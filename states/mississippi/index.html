Mississippi scores its national and 40-question state portions separately, and candidates who fail usually leaned too heavily on national material. Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker wants to apply for appointment to MREC. How many years of Mississippi residency are required?

  1. A At least 10 years
  2. B At least 5 years
  3. C At least 3 years
  4. D At least 6 years
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D At least 6 years

To serve on MREC, a person must have been a Mississippi resident for at least 6 years and must have been a licensed broker for at least 5 years. Appointments are made by the Governor with Senate consent.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

MREC issues a subpoena to a broker during an investigation. What can this subpoena compel?

  1. A Only trust account records
  2. B Only financial records
  3. C Only witness testimony
  4. D Witnesses and documents
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Witnesses and documents

MREC has subpoena power to compel both witnesses and documents during formal hearings and investigations. This broad authority helps MREC gather evidence needed to protect consumers.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

An applicant has a non-fraud felony conviction from 7 years ago. How does MREC handle this?

  1. A Must wait 10 years total
  2. B Automatic approval is granted
  3. C Automatic permanent bar applies
  4. D MREC has discretion to approve
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D MREC has discretion to approve

Under §73-35-10(2)(b), felonies within 5 years are an automatic bar. Felonies beyond 5 years that did not involve fraud, dishonesty, breach of trust, or money laundering are no longer an absolute bar — MREC has discretion to evaluate the applicant.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A salesperson wants to upgrade to a broker license. How much active salesperson experience is required?

  1. A 36 months
  2. B 6 months
  3. C 12 months
  4. D 24 months
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C 12 months

Mississippi requires a broker applicant to have at least 12 months of active experience as a licensed salesperson before applying for a broker license, in addition to completing 120 hours of pre-license education.

Q5 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

What is the minimum E&O insurance coverage required per claim in Mississippi?

  1. A $50,000 per claim
  2. B $100,000 per claim
  3. C $250,000 per claim
  4. D $500,000 per claim
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B $100,000 per claim

Mississippi requires all active licensees to carry E&O insurance with a minimum of $100,000 per claim and $100,000 aggregate. This provides consumer protection since Mississippi has no recovery fund.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

Which form must a Mississippi broker present to all prospective residential clients before establishing a working relationship?

  1. A Form B: Listing Agreement Disclosure
  2. B Form A: Working with a Real Estate Broker
  3. C Form C: Transaction Summary Notice
  4. D Form D: Brokerage Services Addendum
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Form A: Working with a Real Estate Broker

Mississippi requires Form A ('Working with a Real Estate Broker') to be presented to all prospective clients in residential transactions. This form explains the types of agency relationships available.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

Under Mississippi law, dual agency is permitted only when which condition is met?

  1. A The transaction is under $250,000
  2. B The broker notifies MREC in advance
  3. C One party gives verbal consent
  4. D Both parties give written consent
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Both parties give written consent

Mississippi permits dual agency ONLY with the written consent of both parties. Verbal consent or consent from just one party is insufficient. The MREC Dual Agency Confirmation Form must be used.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

Who is responsible for supervising all salespersons in a Mississippi brokerage?

  1. A Each salesperson supervises themselves
  2. B The responsible broker
  3. C The office manager on duty
  4. D The MREC compliance officer
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B The responsible broker

The responsible broker must supervise all salespersons associated with the brokerage. This is a non-delegable duty under Mississippi law, and the broker is accountable for associates' actions within the scope of their work.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

By when must a Mississippi broker deposit earnest money into the trust account?

  1. A Within 5 calendar days of receipt
  2. B Close of business on the next banking day
  3. C Within 3 business days of receipt
  4. D Within 24 hours of contract acceptance
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Close of business on the next banking day

Mississippi has one of the shortest deposit windows in the US. Earnest money must be deposited by close of business on the NEXT BANKING DAY after receipt. This strict timeline protects buyer funds.

Q10 of 20 State Practice

A Mississippi salesperson places a newspaper ad featuring only the property address and her personal phone number. What violation has occurred?

  1. A The ad needs the property's legal description
  2. B The ad should include the MLS number
  3. C No violation if the salesperson is licensed
  4. D The ad lacks the broker's name and phone
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The ad lacks the broker's name and phone

Mississippi requires all advertising to prominently display the broker or firm name AND telephone number. Omitting either is a blind ad violation under MREC rules.

Q11 of 20 State Practice

A Mississippi salesperson closes a transaction. Through whom must she receive her commission?

  1. A Her responsible broker
  2. B The closing attorney
  3. C MREC's escrow office
  4. D The seller directly
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Her responsible broker

In Mississippi, all compensation must flow through the licensee's responsible broker. A salesperson may never receive commission directly from a client or another broker.

Mississippi's Property Condition Disclosure requirements (§§89-1-507 to 89-1-527) apply to residential property sales involving 1 to 4 dwelling units.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

An out-of-state broker wants to sell property in Mississippi. Can she act independently?

  1. A Only for commercial transactions
  2. B Yes, if she holds a principal broker license
  3. C No, she must cooperate with a MS broker
  4. D Yes, with a temporary MREC permit
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C No, she must cooperate with a MS broker

Mississippi is a cooperative state. Out-of-state licensees cannot act independently and must work in cooperation with a Mississippi-licensed broker.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

How long must a Mississippi broker retain transaction records?

  2. B 7 years
  3. C 3 years
  4. D 5 years

Correct: C 3 years

Mississippi requires brokers to retain all transaction records for a minimum of 3 years. This includes contracts, listing agreements, closing statements, correspondence, and trust account records.

Q15 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which activity is MREC authorized to perform?

  1. A Approve mortgage loan terms
  2. B Inspect broker trust accounts
  3. C Regulate title insurance premiums
  4. D Set commission rates for agents
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Inspect broker trust accounts

MREC has the power to inspect broker trust accounts to ensure proper handling of client funds. MREC does NOT have authority to set commission rates, which are determined by the market and negotiated between parties.

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A licensee receives notice that MREC is holding a formal hearing. What type of proceeding does MREC conduct?

  2. B Binding arbitration panels
  3. C Criminal court trials
  4. D Civil jury trial cases

MREC conducts formal administrative hearings when investigating potential license law violations. These hearings follow due process requirements, including notice and the opportunity to present evidence and testimony.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A felony involving fraud or dishonesty occurred 10 years ago. What is the licensing consequence?

  1. A Discretionary review by MREC
  2. B No bar after 5 years
  3. C Permanent bar from licensure
  4. D Required additional education only
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Permanent bar from licensure

Under §73-35-10(2)(b), felonies involving fraud, dishonesty, breach of trust, or money laundering are a permanent bar regardless of when they occurred. The 5-year discretion only applies to non-fraud felonies.

Q18 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A 66-year-old licensee has been licensed for 20 years. Does she qualify for the senior CE exemption?

  1. A No, she needs 25 years licensed
  2. B No, the minimum age is 70
  3. C Yes, age 65 is sufficient
  4. D Yes, 20 years meets the requirement
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A No, she needs 25 years licensed

The senior CE exemption requires BOTH age 65+ AND 25+ years of licensure. While she meets the age requirement at 66, she has only 20 years of licensure, falling short of the 25-year requirement.

Q19 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker's E&O policy has a $3,000 deductible for damages. Does this comply with Mississippi requirements?

  1. A No, maximum deductible is $2,500
  2. B Yes, deductibles are unregulated
  3. C No, maximum deductible is $1,000
  4. D Yes, up to $5,000 is allowed
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A No, maximum deductible is $2,500

Mississippi limits the E&O insurance deductible to a maximum of $2,500 for damages and $1,000 for defense costs. A $3,000 damages deductible exceeds the $2,500 maximum and is non-compliant.

Q20 of 20 State Practice

A Mississippi broker agrees to represent both the buyer and the seller in a transaction with written consent. What type of agency is this?

  1. A Transaction brokerage
  2. B Disclosed dual agency
  3. C Designated agency
  4. D Implied agency
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Disclosed dual agency

Disclosed dual agency occurs when a broker represents both the buyer and seller in the same transaction with the informed written consent of both parties. Mississippi recognizes seller's agents, buyer's agents, disclosed dual agents, and subagents. It does not recognize 'transaction brokerage' or 'designated agency.'

FAQ

Mississippi real estate exam — questions answered

How many questions are on the Mississippi real estate exam?

The Mississippi Real Estate Salesperson Examination has 120 scored multiple-choice questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question Mississippi state-law portion. PSI delivers both portions in one computer-based session and mixes in five to ten unscored experimental questions that look just like the scored ones, so the time spent on those does not count against you.

What's the passing score for the Mississippi real estate exam?

The two portions are scored separately. You need 70 percent on the national portion, which is 56 of 80, and 75 percent on the Mississippi portion, which is 30 of 40. If you pass one portion and fail the other you only retake the portion you failed, but both portions must be passed within the same two-month eligibility window after MREC approves your application.

How much does the Mississippi real estate exam cost?

PSI charges $75 to sit the salesperson exam, and that one fee covers both portions. A retake is also $75, whether you are re-sitting a single portion or both. After you pass, the salesperson license fee paid to MREC is $120. Registration fees are not refundable and expire one year after you register.

How long is the Mississippi real estate exam?

Four hours total. PSI allows two and a half hours for the 80-question national portion and ninety minutes for the 40-question Mississippi portion, taken back-to-back in one sitting. PSI runs a Mississippi test center in Jackson, with additional sites in Memphis, Mobile, and the New Orleans area, and your unofficial score report prints before you leave.

Who administers the Mississippi real estate exam?

The Mississippi Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered by PSI for the Mississippi Real Estate Commission (MREC). You register and schedule your exam date through PSI.

Is the Mississippi real estate exam hard?

It's a serious test, and the part that catches people off guard is the 40-question Mississippi portion. The national half covers the same principles taught in every state, but the state questions reward candidates who actually studied Mississippi's license law, the Form A agency disclosure, the five recognized agency relationships, and the Property Condition Disclosure Statement. Most people who fail leaned too heavily on national material and underprepared on Mississippi law. Honest reps on Mississippi-specific practice questions are the difference between a first-time pass and a $75 retake.

What's on the Mississippi real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers property ownership, land-use controls, valuation, financing, agency, property disclosures, contracts, transfer of title, the practice of real estate, property management, and real estate math, in the proportions set by PSI's content outline where contracts and agency carry the most weight. The 40-question Mississippi portion covers the powers and duties of MREC, licensing and license maintenance, property condition disclosures, agency disclosure and duties to the parties, out-of-state brokers and developers, trust accounts, broker supervision, records and documents, and advertising rules. Agency disclosure is the single largest state topic, so Form A, dual agency, and designated agency are worth extra attention.
