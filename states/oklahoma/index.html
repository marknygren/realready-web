Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A newly appointed OREC commissioner must meet all of the following qualifications EXCEPT:

  1. A Be confirmed by the Senate
  2. B Be an Oklahoma resident for 3 years
  3. C Hold a current broker license
  4. D Be a U.S. citizen
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Hold a current broker license

Only 5 of the 7 commissioners must be licensed brokers. The school representative and lay person do not need broker licenses. All must be U.S. citizens, 3-year Oklahoma residents, and confirmed by the Senate.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A sales associate leaves one brokerage to join another. What must they do?

  1. A Obtain a new license number from OREC
  2. B Complete 6 additional CE hours
  3. C Notify OREC of the change in affiliation
  4. D Retake the state licensing exam
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Notify OREC of the change in affiliation

Licensees must notify OREC of any change in broker affiliation. The license transfers to the new broker. No additional education or re-examination is required for a transfer.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Who may file a complaint against an Oklahoma real estate licensee with OREC?

  1. A Only licensed real estate professionals
  2. B Any person
  3. C Only the licensee's clients
  4. D Only Oklahoma residents
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Any person

Anyone may file a complaint with OREC, whether or not they are a client, a licensee, or an Oklahoma resident. OREC will investigate all valid complaints.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

In Oklahoma, which of the following persons is exempt from real estate licensing requirements?

  1. A A property manager collecting rent for others
  2. B A friend helping negotiate a sale for a fee
  3. C A consultant paid to find buyers for listings
  4. D An attorney handling a transaction for a client
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D An attorney handling a transaction for a client

Attorneys, property owners dealing with their own property, auctioneers, and court-appointed persons are exempt from licensing. Receiving compensation for real estate services generally requires a license.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

Oklahoma's Broker Relationships Act replaced traditional agency terminology with what model?

  1. A Dual agency model
  2. B Designated agency model
  3. C Brokerage services model
  4. D Transaction broker model
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Brokerage services model

HB 2524 (effective Nov. 1, 2013) eliminated traditional agency terms like agent, subagent, and dual agent. Oklahoma brokers now provide 'brokerage services' rather than acting as agents.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

Under Oklahoma's BRA, which duty is owed to ALL parties in a transaction?

  1. A Informing of expected brokerage costs
  2. B Honesty and reasonable skill and care
  3. C Keeping the party informed of progress
  4. D Advising on property condition repairs
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Honesty and reasonable skill and care

The BRA requires brokers to treat ALL parties with honesty and exercise reasonable skill and care. Informing about costs and keeping informed are additional duties owed only to the party being served.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

Under Oklahoma law, which statement about broker-salesperson relationships is correct?

  1. A Associates negotiate their own compensation with clients
  2. B Associates may operate independently of the broker
  3. C Associates are independently liable for all their actions
  4. D The broker is responsible for associate acts within scope
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The broker is responsible for associate acts within scope

Sales associates act under the supervision and authority of their broker. The broker is responsible for the acts of associates performed within the scope of their license.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

All Oklahoma real estate advertising must include which identifier?

  1. A The property's MLS number
  2. B The sales associate's personal name
  3. C The OREC license number
  4. D The broker's licensed name or trade name
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The broker's licensed name or trade name

Oklahoma requires all advertising to include the broker's licensed name or registered trade name. This ensures the public can identify the responsible brokerage.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

Under Oklahoma's RPCDA, when is a seller required to provide a property disclosure statement?

  1. A For all residential property sales
  2. B Only for properties over 10 years old
  3. C When the seller has occupied the property
  4. D Only when requested by the buyer
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C When the seller has occupied the property

Oklahoma's two-track system requires a disclosure statement when the seller has occupied the property. The disclosure covers appliances, systems, structural components, and condition.

Q10 of 20 State Transactions

Which body develops Oklahoma's standardized real estate contract forms?

  1. A The Oklahoma Legislature
  2. B The Oklahoma Bar Association alone
  3. C The Oklahoma Real Estate Commission alone
  4. D The Contract Form Committee
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D The Contract Form Committee

The 13-member Contract Form Committee develops standardized forms including the Oklahoma Uniform Contract of Sale. It has representatives from OREC (5), OBA (3), and OAR (5).

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

How soon after the contract is signed must trust funds be deposited in Oklahoma?

  1. A Within 3 business days
  2. B By the end of the next business day
  3. C Within 24 hours
  4. D Within 5 business days
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Within 3 business days

Oklahoma requires all trust funds to be deposited no later than 3 business days after the contract is signed. Weekends and holidays do not count as business days.

  3. C Yes, if done for compensation
  4. D Only for commercial properties

Correct: C Yes, if done for compensation

Oklahoma requires a real estate license to manage property for others for compensation. Exemptions exist for owners managing their own property and certain on-site managers.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

Does Oklahoma law set a maximum amount for residential security deposits?

  2. B Yes, 2 months' rent maximum
  3. C Yes, 1 month's rent maximum
  4. D No, there is no statutory maximum

Correct: D No, there is no statutory maximum

Oklahoma has no statutory cap on the amount of security deposits. The landlord and tenant negotiate the deposit amount. However, the deposit must be held in a proper escrow account.

Q14 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which of the following is NOT a power of the Oklahoma Real Estate Commission?

  1. A Adopting rules and regulations
  2. B Investigating complaints against licensees
  3. C Setting commission rates for brokers
  4. D Auditing trust accounts
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Setting commission rates for brokers

OREC has authority to adopt rules, investigate complaints, and audit trust accounts. However, commission rates are freely negotiable and cannot be set by any government body or organization.

Q15 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A sales associate's broker retires and closes the brokerage. What happens to the associate's license?

  1. A It remains active for 90 days
  2. B It is automatically revoked by OREC
  3. C It becomes inactive until transferred
  4. D It transfers to the nearest brokerage
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C It becomes inactive until transferred

When a broker ceases business, associates' licenses become inactive until they transfer to a new broker. The closing broker must notify OREC and ensure all trust funds are properly distributed.

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

During an investigation, OREC discovers potential trust account irregularities. What authority does OREC have?

  1. A Freeze the broker's personal bank accounts
  2. B Immediately revoke the broker's license
  3. C Audit the trust account records
  4. D Transfer trust funds to a state account
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Audit the trust account records

OREC has authority to audit trust accounts at any time as part of an investigation. Revocation requires a formal hearing process, not immediate action.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Owners dealing with their own property are exempt from licensing requirements. The license requirement applies to those performing real estate acts for others for compensation.

Q18 of 20 State Practice

Under Oklahoma's BRA, which traditional term was eliminated from real estate practice?

  1. A Sales associate
  2. B Licensee
  3. C Broker
  4. D Subagent

Correct: D Subagent

The BRA eliminated terms like agent, subagent, dual agent, single-party broker, and transaction broker. The terms broker, licensee, and sales associate remain in use.

Q19 of 20 State Practice

A broker receives a written offer on a listing. Under the BRA, what must the broker do?

  2. B Hold it until reviewing with an attorney
  3. C Present it only if it meets the asking price
  4. D Present the offer timely to the seller
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Present the offer timely to the seller

Brokers must receive, reduce to writing upon request, and present timely all written offers and counteroffers. This is a mandatory duty to all parties unless specifically waived in writing.

  2. B The supervising broker and the associate
  3. C Only the sales associate personally

The broker is responsible for acts of associates within the scope of their license. Both the broker (through supervisory responsibility) and the associate (for their own conduct) may face discipline.

About the exam

More about the Oklahoma real estate exam

The Oklahoma real estate salesperson exam is a tougher test than its reputation suggests. The Oklahoma Real Estate Commission, the state agency most people call OREC, scores its national and Oklahoma state-law portions separately, so a strong national score does nothing to make up for a thin grasp of Oklahoma law. That split is exactly why Oklahoma-specific preparation carries as much weight as the national material.

Oklahoma tests its own rules in ways a national course never touches, from the Oklahoma Broker Relationships Act that replaced traditional common-law agency with statutory broker relationships and the duties a broker owes every party to a transaction, to the powers OREC uses to investigate complaints and discipline licensees under the Oklahoma Real Estate License Code, the Residential Property Condition Disclosure Act with its separate disclosure and disclaimer statements on one to four family homes, the rules on psychologically impacted property, and the trust account and Residential Landlord and Tenant Act provisions a property manager has to follow. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need Oklahoma-specific practice questions.

FAQ

Oklahoma real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the Oklahoma real estate exam?

The Oklahoma salesperson licensing exam has 120 scored questions, split into an 80-question national portion and a 40-question Oklahoma state-law portion. Pearson VUE delivers them as two separate exams and mixes in unscored pretest questions, five on the national portion and ten on the state portion, that look just like the scored ones and do not count toward your result.

What's the passing score for the Oklahoma real estate exam?

You need a scaled 70 percent on each portion, scored separately. The national and Oklahoma portions are graded on their own, so passing one does not offset failing the other. If you clear one portion and miss the other, you only retake the portion you failed, as long as you finish both within the year your Commission approval to test stays valid.

How much does the Oklahoma real estate exam cost?

Pearson VUE charges $75 to sit the salesperson exam, and because Oklahoma discounts two exams booked on the same order as two-for-one, that single $75 covers both the national and the Oklahoma portions when you schedule them together. Retaking just the portion you failed runs the exam fee again. The fee is non-refundable and non-transferable. Passing the exam is separate from the $35 application fee you pay the Oklahoma Real Estate Commission to issue your license.

How long is the Oklahoma real estate exam?

Four hours of testing in total, though the two portions are separate exams. Pearson VUE allows two and a half hours for the 80-question national portion and an hour and a half for the 40-question Oklahoma portion. You can schedule them back to back on the same day or on different days, either at a Pearson VUE test center or through the OnVUE remote online proctored option, and you leave with an official pass or fail score report.

Who administers the Oklahoma real estate exam?

The Oklahoma Real Estate Salesperson Examination is administered by Pearson VUE for the Oklahoma Real Estate Commission (OREC). You register and schedule your exam date through Pearson VUE.

Is the Oklahoma real estate exam hard?

It is a serious test, and the part that catches people off guard is the 40-question Oklahoma portion. The national half covers the same principles taught in every state, but the Oklahoma questions reward candidates who actually studied the Oklahoma Broker Relationships Act, the license code and OREC rules, and the state's disclosure requirements. Most people who struggle leaned too heavily on national material and underprepared on Oklahoma law. Honest reps on Oklahoma-specific practice questions are the difference between a first-time pass and a retake.

What's on the Oklahoma real estate exam?

The 80-question national portion covers property characteristics and land use, ownership and transfer of title, valuation and appraisal, real estate contracts and agency, the practice of real estate, property disclosures and environmental issues, financing and settlement, and real estate math, with contracts and agency carrying the most weight. The 40-question Oklahoma portion is built around four areas, the laws and rules affecting Oklahoma real estate practice and OREC's authority, the Oklahoma Broker Relationships Act, property management under the Residential Landlord and Tenant Act, and disclosures and hazards including the Residential Property Condition Disclosure Act. The laws-and-rules section alone is half the state portion, so it is worth extra attention.
