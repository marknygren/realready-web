Q1 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which action is NOT within NMREC's authority?

  1. A Adopting rules for real estate practice
  2. B Prosecuting criminal violations directly
  3. C Suspending a broker's license
  4. D Issuing cease and desist orders
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Prosecuting criminal violations directly

NMREC can investigate and discipline but works with the Attorney General for criminal prosecution. The Commission itself does not prosecute criminal cases directly.

Q2 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which of the following is grounds for disciplinary action against a New Mexico broker?

  1. A Charging a flat fee instead of a percentage
  2. B Declining to list overpriced property
  3. C Refusing to reduce a commission rate
  4. D Commingling client funds with personal funds
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Commingling client funds with personal funds

Commingling trust funds with personal or operating funds is a clear violation of the Real Estate License Law. Commission rates and listing decisions are business choices, not disciplinary matters.

Q3 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

New Mexico's licensing model differs from most states because it:

  1. A Uses a three-tier license system
  2. B Only licenses commercial brokers
  3. C Has no separate salesperson license
  4. D Requires a law degree for all licensees
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C Has no separate salesperson license

New Mexico uses a broker-only model. There is no salesperson license — entry-level licensees are called associate brokers and work under qualifying brokers.

Q4 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

Which of the following is required before an associate broker license can be activated?

  1. A Passing a qualifying broker exam
  2. B Obtaining E&O insurance coverage
  3. C Registering a trade name with NMREC
  4. D Completing two years of experience
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Obtaining E&O insurance coverage

Before activation, associate brokers must obtain Errors and Omissions insurance and have the certificate on file with NMREC. Trade name registration is a qualifying broker requirement.

Q5 of 20 State Practice

A qualifying broker delegates all trust account management to an associate broker. If funds are mishandled, who bears responsibility?

  1. A The associate broker exclusively
  2. B Both brokers share responsibility equally
  3. C The qualifying broker
  4. D Neither broker if policies were in place
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C The qualifying broker

The qualifying broker is ultimately responsible for trust account management and all brokerage operations, regardless of delegation. Supervision duties cannot be fully delegated.

Q6 of 20 State Practice

The relationship between a qualifying broker and associate broker must be documented in:

  1. A A notarized affidavit
  2. B A verbal understanding only
  3. C A written agreement
  4. D An NMREC-filed registration form
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C A written agreement

NM requires a written agreement between the QB and AB that specifies the nature of the relationship, including whether the AB is an employee or independent contractor.

Q7 of 20 State Practice

In New Mexico, property management activities require:

  1. A No license for residential properties
  2. B A separate property management certificate
  3. C A real estate broker's license
  4. D Only a general business license
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C A real estate broker's license

Property management is a licensed real estate activity in NM. All PM activities must be performed by a licensed broker working under a qualifying broker's auspices.

Q8 of 20 State Practice

In New Mexico, a broker's duty of honesty and reasonable care applies to:

  1. A Only the broker's client
  2. B Only the buyer in a sale
  3. C All parties in a transaction
  4. D Only parties with a written agreement
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C All parties in a transaction

NM law requires brokers to treat ALL parties with honesty, reasonable care, and professional conduct — not just their clients. This is a universal duty regardless of representation.

Q9 of 20 State Practice

When must a broker provide written disclosure of their brokerage relationship to a party?

  1. A Before the closing date only
  2. B Only at the time of contract signing
  3. C At the earliest practicable time
  4. D Within 48 hours of first contact
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: C At the earliest practicable time

NM requires written disclosure of existing brokerage relationships at the earliest practicable time — not waiting until contract signing or closing.

Q10 of 20 State Practice

A seller hires a broker to list and sell their home. The broker represents only the seller. This relationship is called:

  1. A Seller/landlord broker
  2. B Facilitator
  3. C Transaction broker
  4. D Dual agent
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A Seller/landlord broker

When a broker represents only the seller or landlord exclusively, this is a seller/landlord broker relationship with full fiduciary duties owed to the seller.

Q11 of 20 State Transactions

In New Mexico, residential property is assessed for tax purposes at what fraction of market value?

  1. A One-quarter of market value
  2. B Two-thirds of market value
  3. C Full market value
  4. D One-third of market value
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D One-third of market value

NM assesses both residential and non-residential property at one-third (33.33%) of market value. The county assessor determines market value, then calculates the assessed value.

Q12 of 20 State Transactions

Under NM's prior appropriation doctrine, who owns all water in the state?

  1. A The public (the state)
  2. B The county where water flows
  3. C The property owner where water is located
  4. D The federal government
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A The public (the state)

In New Mexico, all water belongs to the public (the state). Private parties acquire the right to use water, not ownership of the water itself.

Q13 of 20 State Transactions

The NM Human Rights Act protects all of the following classes EXCEPT:

  1. A Gender identity
  2. B Spousal affiliation
  3. C Sexual orientation
  4. D Marital status
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: D Marital status

The NM Human Rights Act protects race, color, religion, national origin, ancestry, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, spousal affiliation, and disability. Marital status is not a separately listed NM protected class.

Q14 of 20 State Transactions

A property is located within a Public Improvement District (PID). The owner's tax burden includes:

  2. B Regular property taxes plus PID assessments

PID assessments are in addition to regular property taxes. Property owners in PIDs pay both their normal property taxes and the special PID assessments for infrastructure improvements.

Q16 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

A broker fails to disclose to a buyer that they personally own the property being sold. This is an example of:

Failing to disclose a personal interest in a transaction is an undisclosed conflict of interest — a specific ground for discipline under the Real Estate License Law.

Q17 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

In New Mexico, what is the entry-level real estate license called?

  1. A Salesperson
  2. B Associate broker
  3. C Sales associate
  4. D Licensed agent
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: B Associate broker

In NM's broker-only model, the entry-level license is called associate broker. There is no salesperson designation. The supervisory level is qualifying broker.

Q18 of 20 Licensing & Regulation

To upgrade to qualifying broker, an associate broker must have how many years of active experience?

  1. A 4 years within the prior 5 years
  2. B 2 years within the prior 5 years
  3. C 5 years with no time restriction
  4. D 3 years within the prior 7 years
Show answer & explanation +

Correct: A 4 years within the prior 5 years

To become a qualifying broker who supervises associate brokers, NM requires 4 years of active associate broker experience during the 60-month period immediately preceding the application (16.61.3.10 NMAC). A qualifying broker license that will not supervise other brokers requires only 2 years, but that broker must document 4 years of combined experience before becoming eligible to supervise.

Correct: A Review and approve all contracts

Qualifying brokers must review and approve all contracts and documents prepared by their associate brokers as part of their supervisory responsibility.

About the exam

More about the New Mexico real estate exam

New Mexico scores its national and 50-question state-law portions separately, so a strong national score won't rescue a thin grasp of New Mexico law. The exam is set by the New Mexico Real Estate Commission (NMREC), and New Mexico uses the word "broker" for what most other states call a salesperson license, so the very first license you earn is an associate broker license and the exam covers full broker scope from day one. That is why New Mexico-specific preparation matters as much as the national material.

New Mexico tests its own rules in ways a national course never touches, from the mandatory written broker duties every licensee owes to every party in a transaction, to the brokerage relationship choices of exclusive agency, dual agency, and transaction broker, the qualifying broker who supervises every associate broker in an office, the community property rules that decide how married couples take title, prior-appropriation water rights, the required Public Improvement District and estimated property tax disclosures, and the New Mexico Human Rights Act with its broader list of protected classes. None of that looks like the material a candidate in a neighboring state studies. A generic real estate practice test won't cut it. You need New Mexico-specific practice questions.

FAQ

New Mexico real estate exam: your questions answered

How many questions are on the New Mexico real estate broker exam?

The New Mexico Real Estate Broker Examination has 125 scored multiple-choice questions, split into a 75-question national portion and a 50-question New Mexico state-law portion. PSI also mixes in five to ten unscored pretest questions on each portion that look identical to the scored ones, so you should answer everything.

What's the passing score for the New Mexico real estate broker exam?

You need a score of 75 percent on each portion, and the national and state portions are scored separately. If you pass one portion and fail the other, you only retake the portion you failed, but you have to pass both within 90 calendar days of your first attempt or you start over with both halves.

How much does the New Mexico real estate broker exam cost?

The PSI examination fee is $95 per attempt, paid to the testing company when you schedule your seat. That single fee covers the exam whether you sit for one portion or both. After you pass, the associate broker license application fee paid to the New Mexico Real Estate Commission is $270.

How long is the New Mexico real estate broker exam?

Three hours of seat time total. PSI allows two hours for the 75-question national portion and one hour for the 50-question New Mexico portion, taken in one sitting. You get your official pass or fail score report before you leave the test center.

Who administers the New Mexico real estate exam?

The New Mexico Real Estate Broker Examination is administered by PSI for the New Mexico Real Estate Commission (NMREC). You register and schedule your exam date through PSI.

Is the New Mexico real estate broker exam hard?

Yes, more than most candidates expect. New Mexico is one of a smaller group of states where the entry-level license is the broker license, so the exam tests full broker scope from the start, including the brokerage relationships, the written broker duties disclosure, trust accounts, and the qualifying broker supervision structure rather than just salesperson basics. The 50-question state portion is dense with license law and rule, and a single missed concept on something like the broker duties owed to every party or a required disclosure can pull you under the 75 percent line. Honest practice on New Mexico-specific questions, not generic national content, is what gets people across the line on the first try.

What's on the New Mexico real estate broker exam?

The 75-question national portion covers property ownership and land use, valuation and market analysis, financing, general principles of agency, mandated disclosures, contracts, transfer of title, the practice of real estate, and real estate math. The 50-question New Mexico portion covers the Real Estate Commission's structure and disciplinary powers, the license law and Commission rules under 16.61 NMAC, the mandatory broker duties and brokerage relationships, required disclosures including stigmatized property and Public Improvement Districts, community property and how married couples take title, prior-appropriation water rights, trust accounts and earnest money, and the New Mexico Human Rights Act. New Mexico license law and brokerage relationships carry the heaviest weight on the state portion.

After you finish your 90-hour broker pre-licensing coursework, the highest-leverage move is grinding New Mexico-specific practice questions with honest feedback on every miss.
